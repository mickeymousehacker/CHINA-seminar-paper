Ich habe den Casse BYD. und andere andere unternehmen
This cluster examines how successful automotive firms can be built in China under very different governance and financing models. The focus is not on describing the companies, but on comparing alternative ways of organizing ownership, control, and capability building in a highly uncertain and rapidly evolving market. Central question: How can a successful automotive company be built in China? Which governance and financing structure is superior under which conditions—and which model is most likely to create long-term shareholder value?
Case: BYD BYD pursued a vertically integrated strategy, building capabilities in batteries and electric vehicles internally. The firm illustrates a model based on technological control and deep integration rather than M&A or partnerships.
Key questions: What are the main differences between M&A-driven, joint venture-based, vertically integrated, and capital-market-driven models? How do these structures affect access to technology, capital, and scale? Which risks are borne by whom? Which model is most robust under changing market conditions?
Joint task: Compare the four cases in terms of governance, financing, capability building, and long-term viability. Answer the central question: Which model is most likely to succeed in the long run—and why? Formulate 2–3 general lessons about governance and value creation in emerging high-growth industries.
