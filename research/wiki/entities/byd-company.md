# BYD Company Limited

**The world's largest manufacturer of new-energy vehicles (NEVs) and the archetype of vertically integrated private enterprise in China's automotive industry.**

## Overview

BYD (Build Your Dreams) was founded on 10 February 1995 by [[wang-chuanfu]] in Shenzhen as a rechargeable battery manufacturer with CNY 2.5 million in capital and 20 employees. The company's initial focus was nickel-cadmium (NiCd) batteries, rapidly pivoting to lithium-ion cells in 1996 and securing supply contracts with Motorola (2000) and Nokia (2002). [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

In 2003, BYD entered automotive manufacturing by acquiring Xi'an Qinchuan Automobile Company, deliberately choosing reverse engineering over licensed technology to build a fully integrated supply chain from scratch. This origin story is foundational to the company's culture: capabilities are built internally and owned permanently rather than leased from suppliers. By 2009, automotive revenue exceeded 50% of total revenue; by 2023 it exceeded 80%. In 2022, BYD became the first major automaker to cease production of all pure internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

BYD is dual-listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (002594.SZ) and the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (1211.HK). Ownership is founder-concentrated: [[wang-chuanfu]] holds ~17–17.6% as of early 2026; co-founder [[lu-xiangyang]] controls ~5.11% via Youngy Investment Holding Group. Founding members together hold more than one-third of voting rights. The company follows a one-share-one-vote structure. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

In 2024, BYD delivered 4.27 million new-energy vehicles, capturing 34.1% of China's EV market and ~22% of the global NEV market — surpassing [[tesla]] in global BEV sales for the first time. Revenue reached CNY 777.1 billion ($107.87 billion). BYD's 2024 gross margin hit a record 19.4%, though 2025 brought margin compression to 10–15% amid an intensifying domestic price war. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
