# Wang Chuanfu

**Founder, Chairman, and CEO of [[byd-company]]; the largest individual shareholder and the architect of BYD's vertically integrated, technology-first strategy.**

## Overview

Wang Chuanfu was born in 1966 in Anhui Province and trained as a chemist and materials scientist, working as a vice supervisor at the Beijing Nonferrous Research Institute before founding BYD at age 29. He spotted an opportunity in 1994 when Japanese battery manufacturers began shifting from NiCd to NiMH and Li-ion cells, leaving a gap that a low-cost Chinese entrant could fill. He assembled a team of 20 people, raised CNY 2.5 million, and founded Shenzhen BYD Battery Company Limited on 10 February 1995. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

Wang's scientific background deeply shaped BYD's corporate culture. His approach to capability building relied on reverse engineering competitor products rather than licensing technology, establishing the internal manufacturing of high-cost components as a first principle. When [[byd-company]] entered automotive manufacturing in 2003, Wang applied this same philosophy: acquire the shell (Xi'an Qinchuan Automobile), then build every critical subsystem in-house. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

As of early 2026, Wang holds approximately 17–17.6% of BYD Company Limited, making him the single largest individual shareholder. Combined with co-founder [[lu-xiangyang]]'s ~5.11%, the founding team controls more than one-third of total voting rights — a concentration that enables unusually fast strategic decision-making. Wang has explicitly named speed as a competitive weapon: "a fast decision-making mechanism is one of BYD's important advantages… companies must have three abilities to win: core technology, precise strategic direction, and fast decision-making mechanism." [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
