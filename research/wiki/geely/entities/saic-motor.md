# SAIC Motor

**China's largest state-owned automaker, known for its joint-venture model with General Motors (SAIC-GM, est. 1997) and Volkswagen (SAIC-VW, est. 1984), representing the dominant alternative to Geely's M&A-driven approach.**

## Overview

SAIC Motor Corporation Limited is a state-owned enterprise headquartered in Shanghai. Its business model is built around equity joint ventures with Western OEMs: SAIC-Volkswagen (founded 1984; SAIC 50%, VW AG 40%, VW China Invest 10%) and SAIC-GM (founded 1997, initially 50:50) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

These JVs historically delivered large equity-accounted profits and dividends that shaped SAIC's ROE. SAIC-VW revenues reached ~RMB 159bn in 2021 (though -8.7% YoY). However, the JV model has shown structural vulnerability in the EV transition: SAIC-GM volumes collapsed from 1.4 million vehicles (2020) to 435,000 (2024) as Chinese EV competitors displaced Western combustion-engine lineups [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

Volkswagen and SAIC signed an extension of their JV agreement to 2040 and are investing to accelerate transformation toward EVs and connected vehicles. The CSIS case study ("China's Competitiveness — SAIC Motor Corporation") provides detailed governance analysis of the JV model [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

## Key facts
- SAIC-Volkswagen: founded 1984; equity SAIC 50%, VW AG 40%, VW China Invest 10%; JV extended to 2040 [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- SAIC-GM: founded 1997 (as Shanghai GM); currently 50:50; SAIC briefly held 51% (Feb 2010–Apr 2012) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- SAIC-VW revenues 2021: ~RMB 159bn (−8.7% YoY) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- SAIC-GM volume: ~1.4m units (2020) → ~435k units (2024) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- State-owned enterprise; governance contrasts with ZGH's private, founder-controlled model [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
