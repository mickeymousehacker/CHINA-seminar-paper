# Zhejiang Geely Holding Group

**The private Chinese automotive conglomerate wholly owned by Li Shufu that serves as the ultimate holding company for all Geely-branded businesses, Volvo Cars, Polestar, Lotus, LEVC, Smart JV, and other assets.**

## Overview

Zhejiang Geely Holding Group (ZGH) is a private company incorporated in the People's Republic of China and wholly owned by [[li-shufu]] (Eric Li). It is the apex of the Geely corporate pyramid and sits above the publicly listed [[geely-automobile-holdings]] (HK: 0175) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

ZGH's portfolio spans multiple listed and unlisted entities. Listed subsidiaries/associates include Geely Automobile Holdings (HK: 0175), Volvo Cars AB (Nasdaq Stockholm, IPO October 2021), Polestar (Nasdaq US), Lotus Technology (Nasdaq US), and ZEEKR (NYSE). Unlisted holdings include LEVC (London Electric Vehicle Company), Proton (Malaysia), Lynk & Co, Farizon Auto (commercial vehicles), and a 50:50 JV for the Smart brand with Mercedes-Benz [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

Between 2010 and 2021 ZGH executed a systematic cross-border acquisition and joint-venture programme: Volvo Cars (2010, $1.8bn), LEVC (2013), Proton/Lotus (2017), Smart JV with Daimler (2020), Lynk & Co JV with Volvo (2017), and a battery R&D JV with CATL (2018) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

The holding structure uses family trusts and holding vehicles to maintain control continuity. The board of ZGH nominates directors to the listed Geely Auto board, preserving Li Shufu's strategic direction across all entities [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

## Key facts
- Privately held; 100% owned by Li Shufu [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Largest shareholder of Geely Auto via ZGH (39.94%) + Proper Glory Holding Inc. (26.2%) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Completed Volvo Cars acquisition August 2, 2010 for USD 1.8bn [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Acquired LEVC 2013; acquired 51% of Lotus Cars May 2017 from DRB-HICOM [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Formed Smart Automobile Co., Ltd. JV with Daimler/Mercedes-Benz in January 2020 (50:50, RMB 5.4bn registered capital) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Signed Lynk & Co JV agreement August 2017 with Geely Auto and Volvo Car Group [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Established battery R&D JV with CATL in 2018 [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Chinese government provided ~$932m (~50% of total capital raised) for Volvo acquisition financing [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
