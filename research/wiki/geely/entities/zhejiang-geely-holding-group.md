# Zhejiang Geely Holding Group

**The private Chinese automotive conglomerate wholly owned by Li Shufu that serves as the ultimate holding company for all Geely-branded businesses, Volvo Cars, Polestar, Lotus, LEVC, Smart JV, and other assets.**

## Overview

Zhejiang Geely Holding Group (ZGH) is a private company incorporated in the People's Republic of China and wholly owned by [[li-shufu]] (Eric Li). It is the apex of the Geely corporate pyramid and sits above the publicly listed [[geely-automobile-holdings]] (HK: 0175) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

ZGH's portfolio spans multiple listed and unlisted entities. Listed subsidiaries/associates include Geely Automobile Holdings (HK: 0175), Volvo Cars AB (Nasdaq Stockholm, IPO October 2021), Polestar (Nasdaq US; in distress, going-concern 2023), Lotus Technology (Nasdaq US; revenue –44% FY2025), and ZEEKR (NYSE, listed May 2024; privatised December 2025). Unlisted holdings include LEVC (London Electric Vehicle Company), Proton (Malaysia; 49.9%, consolidated into Geely Auto July 2023), Lynk & Co (now 51% ZEEKR / 49% Geely Auto), Farizon Auto (commercial vehicles), and a 50:50 JV for the Smart brand with Mercedes-Benz [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

Between 2010 and 2020, ZGH executed a systematic cross-border acquisition and JV programme: Volvo Cars (2010, USD 1.8bn), LEVC (2013), Proton/Lotus (2017), Smart JV with Daimler (2020), Lynk & Co JV with Volvo (2017), and a battery R&D JV with CATL (2018) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]. Since 2020, the portfolio has shifted toward capital market monetisation (listing ZEEKR, Polestar, Lotus Technology on US/European exchanges) while managing significant losses at multiple portfolio companies.

The holding structure uses family trusts and holding vehicles to maintain control continuity. The board of ZGH nominates directors to the listed Geely Auto board, preserving Li Shufu's strategic direction across all entities [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

ZGH increasingly acts as lender of last resort for its loss-making subsidiaries — pledging guarantees for Polestar's bank facilities and backstopping Lotus Technology's obligation to absorb 100% of Lotus UK (following put option exercises by both Geely and Etika in 2025) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]].

## Key facts

- Privately held; 100% owned by Li Shufu [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Largest shareholder of Geely Auto via ZGH (39.94%) + Proper Glory Holding Inc. (26.2%) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Completed Volvo Cars acquisition August 2, 2010 for USD 1.8bn [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Acquired LEVC 2013; acquired 51% of Lotus Cars May 2017 from DRB-HICOM [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Proton: 49.9% stake acquired July 2017; Proton consolidated into Geely Auto balance sheets July 2023 [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Formed Smart Automobile Co., Ltd. JV with Daimler/Mercedes-Benz in January 2020 (50:50, RMB 5.4bn registered capital) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Signed Lynk & Co JV agreement August 2017 with Geely Auto and Volvo Car Group; Lynk & Co restructured November 2024 (ZEEKR 51% / Geely Auto 49%) [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]]
- Established battery R&D JV with CATL in 2018 [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Chinese government provided ~USD 932m (~50% of total capital raised) for Volvo acquisition financing [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]]
- Acts as guarantor for Polestar bank facilities; ZGH backstops Lotus Technology Lotus UK acquisition obligation [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]]
