# Proton Holdings

**Malaysian national automaker; 49.9% owned by Geely Auto (via ZGH), 50.1% by DRB-HICOM; formally consolidated into Geely Auto's balance sheets in July 2023.**

## Overview

Proton Holdings Berhad (Proton Holdings Berhad, registration 623177-A) is Malaysia's largest domestic automaker, known for producing the Proton Saga and other national vehicles. Zhejiang Geely Holding Group entered into a definitive agreement in July 2017 to acquire a 49.9% stake from DRB-HICOM, with DRB-HICOM retaining 50.1% majority control [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

The deal brought three Geely nominees onto Proton's board: Daniel Donghui Li (CFO ZGH), Dr Nathan Yuning (EVP International Business), and Feng Qing Feng (Group VP and CTO, Geely Auto). Li Chunrong, appointed CEO in September 2017, became the third foreigner to lead Proton [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

In July 2023, Proton was formally consolidated into Geely Auto's consolidated balance sheets — a significant accounting event that expands Geely Auto's reported scope without changing the economic ownership percentages. The 49.9%/50.1% split between Geely Auto and DRB-HICOM is unchanged [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

Proton produced 154,611 vehicles in FY2023, contributing to the Geely group's total disclosed volume [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]. The partnership aims to position Proton as a major player in the ASEAN automotive market across Brunei, Indonesia, Singapore, Thailand, and Malaysia.
