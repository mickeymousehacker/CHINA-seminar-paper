# Polestar

**Swedish-Chinese premium EV brand, jointly controlled by Zhejiang Geely Holding Group and Volvo Cars; listed on Nasdaq US (PSNY) via SPAC merger in June 2022; in severe financial distress as of FY2023 with a going-concern qualification.**

## Overview

Polestar emerged from Volvo Cars' performance division and was spun out as a standalone EV brand under joint ZGH/Volvo Cars ownership. It listed on Nasdaq US under PSNY in June 2022 via a SPAC merger with Gores Guggenheim, making FY2022 its first full reporting year as a public company. Polestar uses an asset-light manufacturing model, producing vehicles in Volvo- or Geely-controlled facilities in China, the US, and South Korea [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

In FY2022, Polestar reported revenue of USD 2.44bn and a net loss of USD 477m (gross profit: USD 98.4m; gross margin: approximately +4%). This was its strongest gross-profit performance before a sharp deterioration in FY2023 [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]].

FY2023 was deeply troubled. Revenue fell slightly to USD 2.38bn (FY2022: USD 2.44bn) while the net loss widened sharply to USD 1.19bn (FY2022: USD 477m). Gross profit turned negative at –USD 414.7m (gross margin: –17.4%), driven by approximately USD 450m in non-cash impairment charges on Polestar 2 assets and inventory. Auditor Deloitte issued a going-concern qualification for FY2023, and management acknowledged "significant doubt" about the company's ability to continue without executing its liquidity plan [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

Ownership as of June 2025 is highly fragmented. PSD Investment (Li Shufu's personal entity) holds approximately 44% (following a USD 200m investment in June 2025 that increased the combined Geely/Li Shufu stake to ~66%); Geely Holding holds approximately 22% directly; and Volvo Cars holds approximately 16% (reduced from 48% in February 2024). Public shareholders hold approximately 18% [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

Recovery actions included securing USD 800m in 12-month bank term facilities and targeting a further USD 400m+ facility (January 2025), with ZGH pledging support as guarantor. Total debt burden was approximately USD 5.1bn, with cash reserves falling from USD 418.9m (end-2023) to USD 103.1m (end-2024) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

Polestar's H1 2024 deliveries fell 27% YoY to 20,371 units, reflecting continued demand challenges in the premium EV segment despite the launching of Polestar 3 and 4 models [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]].
