# Lynk & Co

**Chinese-European automotive brand established as a JV in August 2017 among ZGH, Geely Auto, and Volvo Car Group; equity restructured November 2024 to ZEEKR 51% / Geely Auto 29% / ZGH 20%; fully integrated into the ZEEKR group February 2025; FY2025 sales ~350,495 units (+22.8% YoY); European distribution handed to Volvo Cars March 2026.**

## Overview

Lynk & Co was established by a JV agreement signed in August 2017 between ZGH, Geely Automobile Holdings, and Volvo Car Group. The brand deploys the [[cma-platform]] co-developed by Geely and Volvo and is positioned between mainstream Geely and premium Volvo in the brand hierarchy [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

The original equity structure was: Geely Auto 50%, Volvo Car Group 30%, ZGH 20%. In November 2024, this was restructured: [[zeekr]] acquired Volvo's 30% stake (for CN¥ 5.4bn) plus 21% from Geely Auto (for CN¥ 3.6bn), giving ZEEKR 51% of Lynk & Co while Geely Auto retained 29% (50% − 21% = 29%) and ZGH retained its original 20%. This consolidation placed Lynk & Co under the ZEEKR premium-EV group umbrella [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]. Full operational integration of Lynk & Co into the ZEEKR group was completed in February 2025 [[2026-06-19-geely-h1-2025-interim-results]].

Lynk & Co has shown consistent sales growth in China: 180,127 units in FY2022, 220,250 in FY2023 (+22%), 285,441 in FY2024 (+30%), and approximately 350,495 in FY2025 (+22.8%) [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]] [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]].

In Europe, the brand had operated via subscription centres, offering plug-in hybrids and BEVs exclusively in seven markets (Sweden, Netherlands, Belgium, Germany, Spain, France, Italy). The subscription model accumulated approximately SEK 1bn / EUR 100m in losses between 2020 and 2021. In March 2026, a strategic shift was announced: Volvo Cars will serve as the exclusive importer of Lynk & Co vehicles in Europe, using Volvo's existing retailer network, effectively reducing Lynk & Co's standalone European operating costs [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]].
