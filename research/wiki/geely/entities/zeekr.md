# ZEEKR

**Premium Chinese EV brand launched by Zhejiang Geely Holding Group; NYSE listed May 2024 (ticker: ZK); subsequently taken private and fully absorbed into Geely Auto in 2025; now holds 51% of Lynk & Co.**

## Overview

ZEEKR is ZGH's premium all-electric brand targeting the upper segment of the Chinese EV market. It was positioned to compete with NIO, BMW, and Tesla. Its vehicles — including the ZEEKR 001, 009, and X models — are built on Geely's proprietary [[sea-platform]] (Sustainable Experience Architecture) [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

ZEEKR launched commercially in April 2021 with the ZEEKR 001 as its debut model. FY2022 was its first full calendar year of deliveries, during which it delivered 71,941 vehicles — a dramatic increase from the approximately 6,007 units delivered in the partial launch year of 2021 [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]. All deliveries in FY2022 were pure-electric vehicles on the SEA platform. At this stage ZEEKR was a wholly consolidated (unlisted) subsidiary of Geely Automobile Holdings.

As of 31 December 2023, Geely Automobile Holdings held 62.8% of ZEEKR, with the remaining shares held by management, co-investors, and strategic partners. ZEEKR completed its NYSE IPO in May 2024 (ticker: ZK), raising approximately USD 441m — described as the largest Chinese company NYSE IPO since 2021 — at an implied valuation consistent with the February 2023 funding round valuation of USD 13bn [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

By July 2025, Geely Auto had completed a merger acquiring all remaining public shares, and ZEEKR's privatisation was completed in December 2025, making it a wholly owned subsidiary of Geely Automobile Holdings once again [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]].

ZEEKR's FY2023 financial results were loss-making at the operating level: revenue USD 7.28bn, operating loss USD 1.15bn, and net loss USD 1.16bn. However, delivery volumes grew strongly — from 71,941 in FY2022 to 118,685 in FY2023 (+65% YoY), then to 222,123 in FY2024 [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]].

Following the privatisation, Lynk & Co was restructured so that ZEEKR holds 51% and Geely Auto 49% of Lynk & Co shares (completed November 2024), rationalising the mid-to-premium EV portfolio under a single ZEEKR/Lynk & Co umbrella [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2025]].
