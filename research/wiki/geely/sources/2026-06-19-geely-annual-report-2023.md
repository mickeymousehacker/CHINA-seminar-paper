# Geely Automobile Holdings FY2023 Annual Report

**The FY2023 Annual Report of Geely Automobile Holdings Limited (HK: 0175) covers the financial year ended 31 December 2023, reporting record revenue of ~USD 25.32bn alongside a total group sales volume exceeding 1.68 million vehicles, while detailing the Galaxy brand launch, Geometry consolidation, Proton integration, and the Smart Geely 2025 technology strategy.**

## Key takeaways

- FY2023 revenue reached approximately USD 25.32bn (+15% YoY from FY2022's USD 21.99bn), a new record for the listed group, though net profit attributable to equity holders was broadly flat at ~CNY 4.95bn (FY2022: ~CNY 4.94bn).
- Group vehicle sales exceeded 1.68 million units in FY2023, driven by strong growth in ZEEKR (+65% to 118,685 units) and Lynk & Co (+22% to 220,250 units), with the newly launched Galaxy sub-brand contributing 83,497 units in its first partial year.
- The Galaxy NEV lineup was unveiled in February 2023 as Geely Auto's mass-market new energy vehicle platform (PHEV-focused), and the Geometry brand was simultaneously consolidated into Geely Auto as its entry-level electric series in March 2023.
- Proton Holdings (49.9% Geely Auto stake) was formally consolidated into Geely Auto's balance sheets in July 2023, adding 154,611 units of annual production to the disclosed group perimeter.
- The company continued executing the "Smart Geely 2025" strategy — targeting 3.65 million vehicle sales annually by the end of the strategy period — built on seven technology pillars including intelligent driving, intelligent cockpits, SEA/CMA electric architecture, and battery systems.

## Claims

- FY2023 revenue was approximately USD 25.32bn, an increase of approximately 15% from FY2022's USD 21.99bn, representing a new annual record for the listed Geely Automobile Holdings entity. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- FY2023 net income was approximately CNY 4.945bn (~USD 700–750m), broadly in line with FY2022 and substantially lower than FY2024's RMB 16.8bn (+240%). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Total group vehicle sales in FY2023 exceeded 1.68 million units, comprising: Geely brand 1,034,737; Geometry 191,346; Galaxy 83,497 (partial year); Lynk & Co 220,250; ZEEKR 118,685. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- ZEEKR delivered 118,685 vehicles in FY2023, up approximately 65% from 71,941 in FY2022, positioning it as the fastest-growing brand in the Geely Auto stable. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Lynk & Co delivered 220,250 vehicles in FY2023, up from 180,127 in FY2022 (+22% YoY). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- The Galaxy NEV sub-brand was unveiled in February 2023 as Geely Auto's mass-market plug-in hybrid and pure electric product line, contributing 83,497 units in its first partial year of sales. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- The Geometry brand was consolidated into Geely Auto in March 2023 as Geely Geometry, becoming the entry-level electric product series within the Geely brand umbrella. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Proton Holdings (Malaysia; 49.9% owned by Geely Auto since 2017) was formally consolidated into Geely Auto's balance sheets in July 2023; Proton produced 154,611 units in FY2023. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Smart Automobile Co., Ltd. (50:50 ZGH/Mercedes-Benz JV) sold approximately 70,000 vehicles globally in FY2023, with China monthly volumes falling below 2,000 units in H2 2023, prompting significant price cuts (Smart #1: –CNY 24,100; Smart #3: –CNY 45,000). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Geely Auto held 62.8% of ZEEKR as of end-2023, ahead of ZEEKR's NYSE IPO in May 2024 (ticker: ZK; raised ~USD 441m; largest Chinese company NYSE IPO since 2021). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- As of 31 December 2023, ZGH held ~39.94% of Geely Auto directly, Proper Glory Holding Inc. held ~26.2%, and BlackRock held ~4.57%; single-class shares (one vote per share). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- The "Smart Geely 2025" strategy, launched in 2021, targets 3.65 million vehicle sales annually and is structured around seven technology pillars: intelligent driving, intelligent cockpits, electronic/SEA architecture, battery systems, powertrain, manufacturing technology, and safety. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Geely Auto operates 13 manufacturing plants in China with combined annual capacity of approximately 2.61 million vehicles and employs approximately 73,000 staff as of end-2023. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Geely brand vehicles are sold in 86 countries; ZEEKR operates 689 stores globally (537 in China, 152 internationally); Lynk & Co operates 378 centres in China and networks in 25 countries/regions. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]
- Polestar (jointly controlled by ZGH and Volvo Cars; Nasdaq: PSNY) reported FY2023 revenue of USD 2.38bn, a net loss of USD 1.19bn, and a going-concern qualification from auditor Deloitte — down from FY2022 revenue USD 2.44bn and net loss USD 477m. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023]]

The $13bn valuation figure (from a February 2023 funding round) should be in the report as a reference point.

**Lynk & Co restructuring:** The November 2024 restructuring (ZEEKR acquiring 51% of Lynk & Co from Volvo's 30% + 21% from Geely Auto) post-dates the FY2023 Annual Report. As of the report date, equity was Geely Auto 50%, Volvo Car Group 30%, ZGH 20%.
