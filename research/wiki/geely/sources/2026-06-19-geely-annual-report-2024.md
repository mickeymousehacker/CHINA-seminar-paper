# Geely Automobile Holdings FY2024 Annual Report

**The FY2024 Annual Report of Geely Automobile Holdings Limited (HK: 0175) covers the financial year ended 31 December 2024, reporting record profit attributable to shareholders of RMB 16.63bn (+213% YoY), record revenue of RMB 240.2bn (~USD 33.4bn, +34% YoY), and approximately 2.17 million group vehicle sales (+32% YoY), while detailing the ZEEKR NYSE IPO, Lynk & Co ownership restructuring, the 26.4% Renault do Brasil stake acquisition, and new technology partnerships with NVIDIA and Vodafone.**

## Key takeaways

- FY2024 profit attributable to shareholders reached a record RMB 16.63bn (+213% YoY from RMB 5.31bn in FY2023), driven by operating leverage, NEV mix shift, and declining raw material costs; total group net profit was approximately RMB 16.8bn (+240%).
- FY2024 revenue reached RMB 240.2bn (~USD 33.4bn), growing approximately 34% YoY from FY2023's RMB ~178.9bn, with basic EPS rising 219% to RMB 1.64 and a dividend payout ratio of 42.03% (dividend per share CNY 0.22; proposed 50% increase in final DPS).
- Group vehicle sales reached approximately 2.17 million units in FY2024 (+32% YoY from 1.68m in FY2023), with ZEEKR the fastest-growing brand (est. ~222k deliveries vs. 118,685 in FY2023); the 2025 sales target was set at 2,710,000 units.
- ZEEKR completed its NYSE IPO in May 2024 (ticker: ZK, raised ~USD 441m) and was subsequently privatised and delisted from NYSE by December 2025, reflecting ZGH's decision to consolidate ZEEKR fully within the private group.
- The Lynk & Co ownership structure was restructured in November 2024: ZEEKR acquired a 51% controlling stake (absorbing Volvo Cars' 30% and 21% from Geely Auto), leaving Geely Auto with 29%; Volvo Cars exited Lynk & Co entirely.
- Geely acquired a 26.4% stake in Renault do Brasil, deepening international expansion beyond Asia; new technology partnerships with NVIDIA/ThunderSoft (AI vehicle systems) and Vodafone (connected EV services) were announced.
- Polestar (jointly ZGH/Volvo Cars controlled; Nasdaq PSNY) remained a material portfolio risk, with cash falling from USD 418.9m (end-2023) to USD 103.1m (end-2024), auditor going-concern qualification in place, and ZGH acting as guarantor for additional credit facilities.

- Group gross margin for FY2024 was approximately 15.88–16.98%, reflecting improved product mix and cost reduction. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]

- ZEEKR completed its NYSE IPO in May 2024 (ticker: ZK) raising approximately USD 441m at a valuation of approximately USD 5bn; ZEEKR was the largest Chinese company to IPO on NYSE since 2021. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- ZEEKR was privatised and delisted from NYSE by December 2025, with ZGH acquiring the outstanding public float and returning ZEEKR to private ZGH ownership. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- In November 2024, ZEEKR acquired a 51% controlling stake in Lynk & Co (comprising Volvo Cars' 30% and 21% from Geely Auto); Volvo Cars fully exited Lynk & Co; Geely Auto retained 29%. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- Geely Automobile Holdings acquired a 26.4% equity stake in Renault do Brasil (Brazil's domestic Renault manufacturing operations), deepening ZGH's presence in emerging automotive markets outside Asia. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- Geely announced a partnership with Vodafone for connected electric vehicle services, integrating Vodafone's IoT/connectivity platform across Geely-brand vehicles. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- Geely announced an AI vehicle systems collaboration with NVIDIA and ThunderSoft aimed at deploying AI-powered intelligent cockpit and autonomous driving assistance features. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- Polestar's cash position fell from USD 418.9m (end-2023) to USD 103.1m (end-2024), a 75% decline; ZGH pledged to act as guarantor for up to USD 400m+ in additional bank credit facilities; total Polestar debt burden was approximately USD 5.1bn. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- ZGH held approximately 39.94% of Geely Automobile Holdings directly; Proper Glory Holding Inc. held approximately 26.2%; BlackRock held approximately 4.57%; Vanguard held approximately 2.49%. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
- Lotus Technology reported FY2024 revenue of approximately USD 924m (+36% YoY) with deliveries of 12,134 vehicles (+70%), though net loss widened to approximately USD 1.075bn and gross margin compressed to approximately 3%. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2024]]
