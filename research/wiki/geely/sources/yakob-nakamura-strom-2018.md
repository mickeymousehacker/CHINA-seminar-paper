# Chinese Foreign Acquisitions Aimed for Strategic Asset-Creation and Innovation Upgrading (2018)

**Journal article reframing Geely-Volvo as strategic asset-creation (not merely asset-seeking): the acquirer generates new innovation capabilities by leveraging the target's technology portfolio.**

## Key takeaways
- Geely-Volvo is not passive absorption of Volvo's assets but active creation of new capabilities that neither firm possessed independently.
- FDI location and utilisation of the target's technology portfolio are key variables determining whether acquisition leads to innovation upgrading.
- Frames the case as "strategic asset-creation and innovation upgrading" — a more dynamic concept than standard asset-seeking FDI.

## Claims
- Geely's acquisition of Volvo is driven by strategic asset-creation motives, not market-seeking or efficiency-seeking FDI.
- Innovation upgrading occurs through deliberate exploitation of the acquired firm's technological portfolio within a new organisational context.
