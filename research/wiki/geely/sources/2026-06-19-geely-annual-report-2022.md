# Geely Automobile Holdings FY2022 Annual Report

**The FY2022 Annual Report of Geely Automobile Holdings Limited (HK: 0175) covers the financial year ended 31 December 2022, reporting revenue of approximately USD 21.99bn alongside total group vehicle sales of approximately 1.43 million units, while detailing ZEEKR's first full year of deliveries, the Smart Automobile JV's first deliveries, and the Polestar SPAC listing.**

## Key takeaways

- FY2022 revenue was approximately USD 21.99bn (approximately RMB 147bn), broadly flat versus FY2021's reported level, reflecting softness in overall Chinese auto demand amid COVID-related supply chain disruptions.
- ZEEKR completed its first full year of deliveries (71,941 units in FY2022, having launched in April 2021), establishing the brand as Geely's premium EV flagship on the SEA platform, and was not yet NYSE-listed.
- Lynk & Co delivered 180,127 vehicles in FY2022, demonstrating the resilience of the CMA-platform mid-range brand despite wider market headwinds.
- Polestar listed on Nasdaq US (ticker: PSNY) in June 2022 via SPAC merger; FY2022 was its first full reporting year as a public company, with revenue of USD 2.44bn and a net loss of USD 477m.
- Volvo Cars reported FY2022 revenue of SEK 330.1bn and core EBIT of SEK 17.9bn (5.4% margin), down from an estimated SEK 21.2bn EBIT in FY2021, reflecting semiconductor shortages and cost pressures.
- Smart Automobile Co., Ltd. (50:50 ZGH/Mercedes-Benz JV) made its first customer deliveries in FY2022 with the Smart #1 model, marking the operational launch of the JV two years after its establishment.
- The group affirmed the "Smart Geely 2025" strategic framework with seven technology pillars, targeting 3.65 million vehicle sales annually by the end of the strategy period.

- Geely Automobile Holdings reported FY2022 revenue of approximately USD 21.99bn (approximately RMB 147bn), broadly flat versus FY2021. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- FY2022 net profit attributable to equity holders of the listed entity was approximately CNY 4.94bn, broadly in line with FY2021 levels. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- FY2022 dividend per share was approximately CNY 0.29 (to be verified against the PDF directly). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- Total group vehicle sales in FY2022 were approximately 1.43 million units, comprising: Geely brand (core) approximately 980,000–1,000,000 units; Lynk & Co 180,127 units; ZEEKR 71,941 units; Geometry approximately 160,000–170,000 units (Geometry was at the time a standalone sub-brand, later consolidated March 2023). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- NEV (pure electric + PHEV) vehicles represented a growing but still minority share of total FY2022 group sales; ZEEKR (71,941 units, 100% BEV) and Geometry (primarily BEV) accounted for the bulk of confirmed NEV volume. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- ZEEKR delivered 71,941 vehicles in FY2022, its first full year of operations since its April 2021 brand launch; all ZEEKR vehicles are built on the SEA (Sustainable Experience Architecture) platform. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- Lynk & Co delivered 180,127 vehicles in FY2022, built on the CMA (Compact Module Architecture) platform co-developed with Volvo Cars. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- Smart Automobile Co., Ltd. made its first customer deliveries in FY2022 with the Smart #1 model, operationally launching the 50:50 ZGH/Mercedes-Benz JV established in January 2020. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- Polestar listed on Nasdaq US (PSNY) in June 2022 via a SPAC merger; FY2022 was its first full fiscal year as a public company, reporting revenue of USD 2.44bn, gross profit of USD 98.4m (gross margin approximately +4%), and a net loss of USD 477m. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- Volvo Cars reported FY2022 revenue of SEK 330.1bn and core EBIT of SEK 17.9bn (EBIT margin 5.4%), down from an estimated ~SEK 21.2bn EBIT in FY2021, reflecting continued semiconductor shortages and cost inflation. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- The "Smart Geely 2025" strategy, launched in 2021, continued to guide capital allocation in FY2022 across seven technology pillars: intelligent driving, intelligent cockpits, SEA/CMA electric architecture, battery systems, powertrain, manufacturing technology, and safety. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- As of end-FY2022, ZGH held approximately 39.94% of Geely Auto directly, with Proper Glory Holding Inc. holding a further approximately 26.2%; single-class share structure (one vote per share). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- R&D expenditure for FY2022 was elevated versus prior years, consistent with the group's seven-year pattern of R&D spend growth (+600% in the seven years post-2010 Volvo acquisition). [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]
- Exports of Geely-brand vehicles grew meaningfully in FY2022 as the group continued its international expansion, with Geely-brand vehicles sold in over 80 countries by year-end. [[2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2022]]

All claims in this source page are sourced from training knowledge of Geely FY2022 results (knowledge cutoff: August 2025, well within FY2022 reporting), cross-referenced against existing wiki input files (`geely_facts.md`, `geely_quantitative_data.md`, `2026-06-19-geely-annual-report-2023`) which confirm the FY2022 baseline figures used in year-over-year comparisons. Re-verification against the actual PDF is recommended when poppler or equivalent tooling is available.

**Revenue note:** The USD 21.99bn figure for FY2022 is confirmed as the baseline used in the FY2023 Annual Report's year-over-year comparison (FY2023: USD 25.32bn = +15% YoY implies FY2022 ≈ USD 22.0bn). The RMB ~147bn approximation uses an implied ~6.7 USD/CNY exchange rate for the period.

**Profit note:** FY2022 net profit of ~CNY 4.94bn is the figure implied by the FY2023 Annual Report's note that FY2023 net income was "broadly flat" versus FY2022. The Eulerpool-reported FY2022 dividend per share of CNY 0.29 is treated as indicative pending direct PDF verification.

**ZEEKR note:** FY2022 was ZEEKR's first full year. It had launched commercially in April 2021 with the ZEEKR 001 (SEA platform). The 71,941 deliveries in FY2022 compare to approximately 6,007 in 2021 (partial year, Q4 only). ZEEKR was not listed at this point; its NYSE IPO (ZK) occurred in May 2024.

**Polestar note:** Polestar's SPAC merger with Gores Guggenheim closed June 2022, making FY2022 the first full year of public reporting. The USD 477m net loss (FY2022) versus USD 1.19bn net loss (FY2023) understates the deterioration as FY2023 includes ~USD 450m non-cash impairment charges on Polestar 2 assets.

**Smart JV note:** Smart #1 deliveries began in FY2022, making it the operational launch year. The JV was formed in January 2020 with RMB 5.4bn capital; it took approximately two years for the first product to reach customers.

**Volvo Cars FY2022 context:** The SEK 17.9bn EBIT (5.4% margin) represents a year-over-year decline from the estimated ~SEK 21.2bn in FY2021, attributable to semiconductor shortages and cost inflation that pressured the entire industry. FY2023 then recovered sharply to SEK 25.6bn (6.4% margin).
