# Geely Automobile Holdings — H1 2025 Interim Results

**Geely Automobile Holdings (HK: 0175) reported H1 2025 revenue of RMB 150.3bn (+27% YoY), total vehicle sales of 1.409 million units (+47% YoY) with NEV share crossing 51.5%, adjusted operating profit of RMB 6.66bn (+102% YoY), and announced ZEEKR privatisation at ~USD 2.4bn total deal value.**

## Key takeaways

- H1 2025 revenue of RMB 150.3bn (+27% YoY) reflects strong volume growth and deepening NEV mix, though gross margin edged down 0.3 percentage points to 16.4%.
- Reported net profit attributable to shareholders fell 14% YoY to RMB 9.29bn, but this is distorted by a one-off RMB 7.73bn gain in H1 2024 from the Volvo Cars / Horse Powertrain transaction; adjusted operating profit of RMB 6.66bn represents a 102% YoY improvement.
- Total vehicle sales of 1,409,180 units (+47% YoY) with NEV volume of 725,151 units (+126% YoY), crossing 51.5% NEV penetration — the first time NEVs accounted for over half of group sales.
- R&D expenditure rose 21% YoY to RMB 7.33bn, underlining continued commitment to technology investment despite margin pressure.
- Balance sheet strengthened in absolute cash terms (RMB 58.8bn), though gearing ratio rose sharply from 8.8% to 22.2% due to debt taken on for the Lynk & Co acquisition from ZEEKR.
- ZEEKR privatisation announced July 2025 (total deal ~USD 2.4bn); Lynk & Co fully integrated into ZEEKR (completed February 2025); annual sales target raised to 3 million units.

- H1 2025 total vehicle sales: 1,409,180 units (+47% YoY).

- Lynk & Co fully integrated into ZEEKR completed February 2025.
- FY2025 annual vehicle sales target raised to 3 million units.

## Notes

Source: Interim Results Announcement for the six months ended 30 June 2025, filed with HKEX on 14 August 2025. Data provided directly by the user from the announcement; no PDF parsing was required.

The H1 2024 reported net profit included a one-off gain of RMB 7.73bn from the Volvo Cars / Horse Powertrain transaction. Adjusted for this, the underlying H1 2025 operating profit improvement of +102% YoY is the more meaningful comparator for assessing recurring earnings power.

The 22.2% gearing ratio (up from 8.8% at end-2024) reflects RMB 19.9bn in borrowings taken on to finance the Lynk & Co acquisition from ZEEKR. Net cash of RMB 38.9bn indicates the group remains net cash positive.

The 51.5% NEV share in H1 2025 is a structural milestone: Geely has crossed the majority-NEV threshold, with 725,151 NEV units in six months representing 126% growth — double the total volume growth rate of 47%.

For BYD/SAIC comparison context: H1 2025 annualised revenue run-rate is approximately RMB 300bn; H1 2025 annualised adjusted operating profit run-rate is approximately RMB 13.3bn. These compare with BYD's FY2024 revenue of approximately RMB 777bn (USD 107bn) and NIO's persistent losses. SAIC H1 2025 comparators not yet ingested into this wiki.
