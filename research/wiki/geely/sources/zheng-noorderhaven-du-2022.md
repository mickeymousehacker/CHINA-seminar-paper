# Making the Unlikely Marriage Work (2022)

**Journal article proposing the four-stage integration model (distancing → balancing → building → diversifying) for Chinese strategic asset-seeking acquisitions, using Geely-Volvo as the primary case.**

## Key takeaways
- External legitimacy pressures (host-country perception of the acquirer) and internal legitimacy pressures (workforce trust) drive the sequencing of integration stages.
- The four-stage model is an emergent, non-linear process shaped by legitimacy dynamics, not a predetermined integration plan.
- Geely's "unlikely marriage" with Volvo succeeded because legitimacy pressures were actively managed at each stage.

## Claims
- Four integration stages: distancing, balancing, building, diversifying.
- Stage transitions are triggered by shifts in legitimacy pressure rather than purely operational logic.
- The model applies to Chinese strategic asset-seeking acquisitions generally, not only to Geely-Volvo.
