# The 'Hybrid' Emerging Market Multinational Enterprise (2014)

**Book chapter arguing that emerging-market firms acquiring developed-market brands via strategic asset-seeking FDI constitute a new category of internationalized company — the "hybrid" EMNE.**

## Key takeaways
- Systematic strategic asset-seeking FDI by EMEs through acquisition of well-established developed-market firms creates a new firm category not captured by traditional MNE theory.
- The Volvo Cars ownership transfer to ZGH is the primary empirical case.
- Hybrid EMNEs combine home-country cost structures with acquired developed-market brand equity and technology.

## Claims
- ZGH's acquisition of Volvo Cars is framed as a paradigm case of hybrid EMNE formation.
- The chapter distinguishes asset-seeking FDI from market-seeking and efficiency-seeking FDI.
