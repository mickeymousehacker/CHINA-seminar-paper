# Geely: A Trajectory of Catching Up and Asset-Seeking Multinational Growth (2012)

**Journal article framing Geely's internationalization as a latecomer catching-up trajectory, culminating in the Volvo acquisition as a deliberate asset-seeking move rather than opportunistic deal-making.**

## Key takeaways
- Geely's progression from reverse-engineering domestic competitors to acquiring international assets (transmissions, then Volvo) follows a classic latecomer catch-up path.
- China became the world's largest automobile market in 2009 after ~35% annual growth 2000–2010.
- The Volvo acquisition represents the culmination of deliberate strategic asset-seeking FDI, not opportunism.

## Claims
- Geely's development is framed as a "trajectory" from reverse engineering → capability building → cross-border M&A.
- Asset-seeking FDI is the primary internationalisation motive, distinct from market- or efficiency-seeking FDI.
