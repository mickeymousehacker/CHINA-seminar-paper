## Synthesized search engine summary (verbatim from search results)

**Deal Structure**

On August 2, 2010, Zhejiang Geely Holding Group completed the acquisition of 100% of Volvo Car Corporation from Ford Motor Company. As announced on March 28, 2010, Geely agreed to pay USD 1.8 billion for Volvo Cars, which included a USD 200 million note with the balance paid in cash. The acquisition included 100% ownership, nine product series, three cutting-edge platforms, over 2,400 global networks, talents, brands, and a crucial supplier system.

**Financing Structure**

Geely raised around $2.7 billion for the Volvo acquisition, of which $1.8 billion was used to acquire 100% of Volvo and $900 million was used to secure subsequent operations of Volvo.

To finance the deal, Geely secured $2.1 billion of loans from Bank of China, China Construction Bank, Export-Import Bank of China, Geely Automobile Holdings (the group's listed arm), and the government of Gothenburg, where Volvo is headquartered. Geely used three main types of financing for its acquisition of Volvo: loans from domestic and foreign banks, equity financing from investors and Ford seller financing.

Additionally, the Chinese government provided essential financing amounting to $932 million, half of the total deal value.

**Financial Impact and Shareholder Value**

Since Volvo was already burdened with $3.5 billion in debt, Geely took over this debt during the acquisition, increasing Geely Group's total debt from 16.05 billion RMB in 2009 to 71.07 billion RMB in 2010. In 2010, Geely Group's capital debt ratio reached 73.47%, compared to the industry's highest at only 60%.

However, in terms of long-term shareholder value, post-deal, Geely's sales and R&D performance surged by 134.5% and 600% respectively after seven years. In the second year of the acquisition, Volvo Cars turned around, with Geely bringing in capital and asset financing, and a revival plan for Volvo got it back up and running.
