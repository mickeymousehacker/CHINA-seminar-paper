## Synthesized search engine summary — Volvo Cars IPO (verbatim from search results)

**IPO Details and Valuation**

The IPO in 2021 priced shares at SEK 53, valuing the company at roughly SEK 158 billion (approximately $18.5 billion) at the time of listing. However, the IPO was priced within a range of 53 to 68 Swedish crowns per share, valuing the automaker at up to $23 billion.

**Stockholm Listing**

Shares began trading on October 28, with the IPO valuing the company between 163 billion and 200 billion crowns.

**Geely Ownership Stake**

Owner Geely remained the biggest shareholder after the listing. The transaction, including expected converted investments by investors AMF and Folksam, was seen resulting in a free float of about 19.5% to 24.0%. This means Geely retained approximately 75-80% ownership of Volvo Cars after the IPO.

**Background**

Geely Holding previously pursued an IPO of Volvo Cars in 2018, but shelved the plans after investors balked at its proposed valuation of as much as $30 billion.

## Synthesized search engine summary — Smart JV (verbatim from search results)

**Joint Venture Formation**

Mercedes-Benz and Geely Holding formally established a global 50:50 joint venture "smart Automobile Co., Ltd." for the smart brand after receiving regulatory approval. The joint venture had a total registered capital of 5.4 billion RMB ($777 million), with 2.7 billion yuan contributed by each partner.

**Governance Structure**

The board of directors of the new smart joint venture consisted of six executives with equal representation from both parties.

Daimler AG board representatives included Hubertus Troska (Member of the Board responsible for Greater China), Britta Seeger (responsible for Marketing & Sales), and Markus Schäfer (responsible for Group Research and Mercedes-Benz Cars Development).

Geely board representatives included Geely Holding Chairman Li Shufu, Geely Holding President and Geely Auto Group President and CEO An Conghui, and Geely Holding Executive Vice President and CFO Daniel Donghui Li.

**Leadership and Operations**

Tong Xiangbei was appointed the new global CEO of the smart joint venture and oversaw all operations relating to the brand including sales, marketing, R&D, production and after sales.

**Location and Development**

The headquarters was located in Hangzhou Bay, Ningbo, China, and the new generation of smart vehicles would be designed by the worldwide Mercedes-Benz Design network and developed by the Geely global engineering network.
