## Synthesized search engine summary (verbatim from search results)

**Academic Research Overview**

Alvstam and Ivarsson (2020) analyze the process of knowledge and technology transfer within an acquired firm after acquisition by a Chinese multinational firm, using the 2010 acquisition of Volvo Car Corporation by Zhejiang Geely Holding Group as their empirical case study.

**Technology Transfer Mechanisms**

The knowledge transfer between Volvo Car and Geely Group includes integrated greenfield plants, collaboration in a new engine plant, coordination and co-operation at supply-systems, integration of management and engineering resources, joint R&D ventures, as well as development based on a joint platform Compact Module Architecture (CMA).

These aspects are used by the Geely Group in order to gradually upgrade their process, product, functional and production capacity, and thereby achieve an upgrading in the global value chain.

**Co-Creation and Innovation**

The findings showed how the high level of freedom given to the acquired firm allowed it to preserve innovation capacity and, later, successfully integrate with the acquirer. Patent portfolio analysis demonstrates that the firms' innovation outputs became more similar over time, demonstrating successful knowledge integration.

**Integration Process Stages**

In the Geely-Volvo case, the integration process proceeded through four stages: distancing, balancing, building, and diversifying.
