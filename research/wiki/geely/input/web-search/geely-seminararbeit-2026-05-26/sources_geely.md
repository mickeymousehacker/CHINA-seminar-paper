## Confirmed Peer-Reviewed Journal Articles

### 1. Balcet, Wang & Richet (2012)
- **Authors:** Balcet, Giovanni; Wang, Hua; Richet, Xavier
- **Year:** 2012
- **Title:** "Geely: A trajectory of catching up and asset-seeking multinational growth"
- **Journal:** *International Journal of Automotive Technology and Management*
- **Volume/Issue:** Vol. 12, No. 4
- **Pages:** 360–375
- **DOI:** 10.1504/IJATM.2012.051361
- **APA:** Balcet, G., Wang, H., & Richet, X. (2012). Geely: A trajectory of catching up and asset-seeking multinational growth. *International Journal of Automotive Technology and Management, 12*(4), 360–375. https://doi.org/10.1504/IJATM.2012.051361
- **Key argument:** Geely's growth from domestic reverse-engineering to international acquisitions is a "latecomer catching up" trajectory; the Volvo deal is the culmination of deliberate asset-seeking FDI, not opportunistic deal-making.

### 2. Yakob, Nakamura & Ström (2018)
- **Authors:** Yakob, Ramsin; Nakamura, H. Richard; Ström, Patrik
- **Year:** 2018
- **Title:** "Chinese foreign acquisitions aimed for strategic asset-creation and innovation upgrading: The case of Geely and Volvo Cars"
- **Journal:** *Technovation*
- **Volume/Pages:** Vol. 70–71, pp. 59–72
- **DOI:** [DOI unknown — confirmed on ScienceDirect via pii S0166497218301160; standard DOI not retrievable without direct access]
- **APA:** Yakob, R., Nakamura, H. R., & Ström, P. (2018). Chinese foreign acquisitions aimed for strategic asset-creation and innovation upgrading: The case of Geely and Volvo Cars. *Technovation, 70–71*, 59–72.
- **Key argument:** Geely-Volvo is asset-*creating*, not merely asset-seeking — the acquirer generates new innovation capabilities by leveraging the target's technology portfolio rather than simply absorbing it.

### 3. Meng & Wang (2021)
- **Authors:** Meng, Ziming; Wang, Xin
- **Year:** 2021
- **Title:** "The effects of outbound mergers and acquisitions (M&As) on Chinese automobile corporations' performance: A case study of Geely's acquisition of Volvo"
- **Journal:** *International Business Research* (Canadian Center of Science and Education)
- **Volume/Issue:** Vol. 14, No. 7
- **Pages:** 1–36
- **DOI:** 10.5539/ibr.v14n7p36
- **APA:** Meng, Z., & Wang, X. (2021). The effects of outbound mergers and acquisitions (M&As) on Chinese automobile corporations' performance: A case study of Geely's acquisition of Volvo. *International Business Research, 14*(7), 1–36. https://doi.org/10.5539/ibr.v14n7p36
- **Key argument:** Geely's post-acquisition financial and operational performance improved measurably over the long run, supporting outbound M&A as a growth mechanism for Chinese automakers.
- **Note:** Published by CCSE; lower-prestige venue than others on this list.

### 4. Zheng, Noorderhaven & Du (2022)
- **Authors:** Zheng, Qiuxia; Noorderhaven, Niels; Du, Jian
- **Year:** 2022
- **Title:** "Making the unlikely marriage work: The integration process of Chinese strategic asset-seeking acquisitions"
- **Journal:** *Journal of World Business*
- **Volume/Issue:** Vol. 57, No. 3
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.jwb.2021.101305
- **APA:** Zheng, Q., Noorderhaven, N., & Du, J. (2022). Making the unlikely marriage work: The integration process of Chinese strategic asset-seeking acquisitions. *Journal of World Business, 57*(3). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jwb.2021.101305
- **Key argument:** Proposes the four-stage integration model (distancing → balancing → building → diversifying) using Geely-Volvo as the primary case; argues that external and internal legitimacy pressures drive the sequencing of integration stages. ← **Confirmed source of the four-stage model** (previously misattributed in wiki to Alvstam & Ivarsson 2020).

### 5. Konda, Slepnikov & Jin (2022)
- **Authors:** Konda, Primoz (Aalborg University); Slepnikov, Dmitrij (Aalborg University); Jin, Jun (Zhejiang University)
- **Year:** 2022
- **Title:** "From transaction to co-creation in Geely's acquisition of Volvo Cars: impact on innovation output and market performance"
- **Journal:** *Asian Journal of Technology Innovation*
- **Volume/Issue:** Vol. 31, No. 3
- **Pages:** 556–580
- **Published online:** 14 October 2022
- **DOI:** 10.1080/19761597.2022.2133777
- **APA:** Konda, P., Slepnikov, D., & Jin, J. (2022). From transaction to co-creation in Geely's acquisition of Volvo Cars: impact on innovation output and market performance. *Asian Journal of Technology Innovation, 31*(3), 556–580. https://doi.org/10.1080/19761597.2022.2133777
- **Key argument:** Only a few EMNEs achieve genuine co-creation with an acquired partner; Geely-Volvo is a rare success case, confirmed via longitudinal patent portfolio and market performance analysis.

- **Year:** 2014
- **Title:** "The 'hybrid' emerging market multinational enterprise — The ownership transfer of Volvo Cars to China"

- **Publisher:** Palgrave Macmillan
- **DOI:** 10.1057/9781137312211_11
- **APA:** Alvstam, C. G., & Ivarsson, I. (2014). The 'hybrid' emerging market multinational enterprise — The ownership transfer of Volvo Cars to China. In C. G. Alvstam, H. Dolles, & P. Ström (Eds.), *Asian Inward and Outward FDI*. Palgrave Macmillan. https://doi.org/10.1057/9781137312211_11
