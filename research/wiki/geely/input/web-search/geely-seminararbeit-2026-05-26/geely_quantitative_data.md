## 5. Polestar (Nasdaq: PSNY) — Financial Crisis 2022–2024

Sources: Polestar investor releases (2023–2024); Polestar FY2023 press release; BusinessWire Q3 2024 update.

| Metric | FY2022 | FY2023 | Q3 2024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | — | — | declining (mid-teens % guidance) |
| Gross profit/loss | USD +98.4m | USD –414.7m | — |
| Gross margin | +4% | –17.4% | ~–17% (FY guidance) |
| Net loss | USD 481.5m | USD 1.17bn | USD 323m (Q3 alone) |
| H1 deliveries | — | — | 20,371 (–27% YoY) |

- FY2023 gross loss includes ~USD 450m non-cash impairment on Polestar 2 assets and inventory
- Auditor Deloitte issued going-concern qualification for FY2023
- Management acknowledged "significant doubt" about ability to continue without executing its liquidity plan
- **Recovery action:** secured USD 800m in 12-month bank term facilities; additional USD 400m+ facility targeted (January 2025); ZGH pledged support as guarantor
- Total debt burden: ~USD 5.1bn
- Analyst estimate (Cantor Fitzgerald): USD 2bn+ additional capital needed through 2028
- Cash reserves: USD 418.9m (end-2023) → USD 103.1m (end-2024); 75% decline
