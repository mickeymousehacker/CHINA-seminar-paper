# Strategic Asset-Seeking FDI

**A form of foreign direct investment in which the investing firm's primary motivation is to acquire strategic capabilities — technology, brands, human capital, or market knowledge — rather than to access markets, resources, or low-cost labour.**

## Summary

Strategic asset-seeking FDI is the theoretical label applied to Geely's cross-border acquisitions in the academic literature. The Technovation (ScienceDirect, DOI 10.1016/j.technovation.2018.xx) article frames Geely-Volvo explicitly as "Chinese foreign acquisitions aimed for strategic asset-creation and innovation upgrading" [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

In this framework, Geely did not buy Volvo Cars primarily to access the Swedish or European market, but to acquire Volvo's engineering capabilities, safety technology, brand equity, and global distribution — assets that took Volvo decades to build and that would have taken Geely a similar period to develop organically. The acquired assets then served as the basis for upgrading Geely's own product and process capabilities via [[technology-transfer]] [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].
