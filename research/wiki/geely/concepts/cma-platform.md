# CMA Platform

**The Compact Module Architecture (CMA) is the shared vehicle platform jointly developed by Geely and Volvo Cars as the flagship product of their post-acquisition technology co-development, deployed across both Volvo-branded and Geely-branded vehicles.**

## Summary

CMA is the tangible engineering output of the Geely-Volvo integration process. It represents the "building" stage of the four-stage integration model (distancing → balancing → building → diversifying) and demonstrates that the arm's-length post-acquisition approach succeeded in creating genuine technological co-development rather than mere brand management [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

The platform was jointly developed by Geely and Volvo engineering teams and is used for multiple vehicles across both brand families. It underpins Volvo's 40-series models as well as Lynk & Co vehicles and certain Geely-brand models. CMA is cited in academic literature as evidence of successful [[technology-transfer]] and of the transition from "transaction" to "co-creation" — Konda, Slepnikov & Jin (2022), "From transaction to co-creation in Geely's acquisition of Volvo Cars," *Asian Journal of Technology Innovation, 31*(3), 556–580, DOI: 10.1080/19761597.2022.2133777 [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].
