# JV Governance

**The governance structures and strategic dynamics of equity joint ventures between Chinese and foreign automotive firms, in which both parties share ownership, control, and profits — typically used as a mechanism for Chinese firms to access foreign technology while foreign firms access the Chinese market.**

## Summary

The JV governance model is best represented in the Chinese automotive industry by [[saic-motor]]'s 50:50 arrangements with [[volkswagen-ag]] (SAIC-VW, founded 1984) and [[general-motors]] (SAIC-GM, founded 1997). These structures historically delivered large equity-accounted profits and dividends. SAIC-VW revenues reached ~RMB 159bn in 2021 [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

However, the JV model has structural weaknesses in the EV transition: it constrains Chinese partner autonomy (technology decisions often require foreign-partner consent), and the foreign partner retains IP ownership. SAIC-GM's volume collapsed from 1.4m (2020) to 435k (2024) as Chinese EV natives displaced JV combustion-engine products [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

The Smart Automobile Co., Ltd. JV (Geely 50:50 with Mercedes-Benz, established 2020) represents Geely's selective use of the JV model for a specific brand — contrasted against Geely's preference for full ownership (Volvo Cars, LEVC) or majority control (Lotus). The Smart JV governance features equal board representation (6 directors, 3 per side) with Li Shufu personally serving as one of Geely's three board nominees [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].
