# Vertical Integration

**A corporate strategy in which a firm owns and controls multiple stages of its supply chain — from raw materials and components to final assembly and distribution — to reduce dependence on external suppliers and capture margins across the value chain.**

## Summary

In the Chinese EV context, [[byd]] is the paradigm case of vertical integration, manufacturing over 70% of its vehicle components in-house including batteries (Blade Battery), power electronics, electric motors, and semiconductors. This strategy is contrasted against Geely's externally sourced capability building via [[cross-border-ma]] and SAIC's dependence on JV partners for technology ([[jv-governance]]) [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

BYD's vertical integration produced exceptional financial results in 2024: revenue of RMB 777bn (+29% YoY), net income of RMB 40.25bn (+34% YoY), and gross margins sustained near 20% despite aggressive pricing competition. It delivered 4.27 million vehicles, surpassing Tesla in plug-in deliveries [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].

The academic literature (ResearchGate/AJMSS: "The Motivation and Economic Consequences of BYD's Vertical Integration Strategy") analyses the strategic rationale: controlling quality and cost throughout the supply chain, eliminating delays from external suppliers, and building a proprietary battery technology moat [[2026-05-22-geely-ma-strategy-web-research]].
