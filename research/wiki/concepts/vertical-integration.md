# Vertical Integration

**A corporate strategy in which a firm controls multiple stages of its value chain internally rather than sourcing from external suppliers, trading flexibility and capital efficiency for cost control, supply security, and integration speed.**

## Summary

Vertical integration exists on a spectrum from pure outsourcing (all components sourced externally) to full vertical integration (all components made in-house). [[byd-company]] sits near the most-integrated extreme, producing approximately 75–80% of its core components through internal subsidiaries — more than double [[tesla]]'s in-house rate. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

BYD's vertical integration covers: batteries (FinDreams Battery / [[blade-battery]]); power semiconductors ([[igbt-semiconductor]] and SiC chips via [[byd-semiconductor]]); permanent magnet motors and e-drive systems (FinDreams Powertrain); automotive electronics and chassis parts (FinDreams Technology); vehicle platforms; and the software stack ([[dilink-xuanji-architecture]]). Raw material sourcing extends to lithium mines in Brazil and refining/cathode production. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

The strategic advantages of BYD's model include: (1) **Cost compression** — industry estimates place BYD's battery costs at RMB 0.4/Wh vs. higher external market prices, enabling 30–40% lower battery costs than competitors; (2) **Supply security** — BYD maintained and increased production during the 2021–2022 global chip shortage while Ford and GM halted lines; (3) **Integration speed** — coordinating R&D across battery, motor, and electronics divisions in-house shortens the development lifecycle and enables faster iteration; (4) **Margin absorption** — the vertically integrated model allows BYD to absorb margin pressure that would cripple competitors dependent on third-party suppliers. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

The risks of deep vertical integration include: (1) **Overcapacity lock-in** — if market demand shifts or technology evolves, fixed assets in underutilized capacity become value-destructive; (2) **External sales conflict** — BYD's battery subsidiary arrangement creates conflict-of-interest concerns that impede FinDreams Battery sales to competing car OEMs; (3) **Capital intensity** — high capex requirements for in-house manufacturing; (4) **Technology bet concentration** — betting on LFP/blade chemistry means exposure if a superior chemistry (e.g., solid-state) renders the existing asset base obsolete. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
