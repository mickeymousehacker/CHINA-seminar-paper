# BYD Vertical Integration Strategy — Web Research (19-angle parallel search)

**19-angle parallel web research on BYD's vertical integration strategy, governance structure, financing model, capability building, and long-term shareholder value, conducted for an academic paper comparing Chinese automotive industry governance models.**

## Key takeaways

- BYD produces 75–80% of its core components in-house — batteries, semiconductors (IGBTs, SiC), motors, powertrain systems, and software — through its [[findreams]] subsidiary brand and [[byd-semiconductor]] unit.
- BYD is a founder-led private enterprise: [[wang-chuanfu]] (17–17.6% stake) and [[lu-xiangyang]] (co-founder, ~5.11% via Youngy Investment) together control >one-third of voting rights, enabling fast unilateral decision-making impossible in JV or SOE structures.
- [[berkshire-hathaway]] invested $230M in 2008 for ~10% stake; it validated BYD's technology trajectory globally and exited in September 2025 after a 3,890% return (20x+).
- BYD's [[blade-battery]] (LFP + CTP cell-to-pack design) is a paradigm-shifting proprietary technology: no cobalt, 5,000+ cycle life, nail-test safe, 439–450 Wh/L volumetric density, 40% fewer secondary components vs. conventional packs.
- 2024 financials: revenue CNY 777.1 billion (+29% YoY); net profit CNY 40.25 billion (+34%); gross margin 19.4% (record high); R&D spend CNY 54.2 billion (+36%); cash CNY 154.9 billion; interest-bearing debt only 4.9% of total liabilities.
- BYD surpassed Tesla in global BEV sales in 2024 (4.27 million NEVs sold, ~41% YoY growth); 34.1% domestic China EV share; ~22% global NEV share.
- Cumulative R&D investment exceeded CNY 180 billion through 2024; in 13 of the past 14 years, BYD spent more on R&D than its net profit — a structural preference for capability reinvestment over dividend distribution.
- Government subsidies matter but are not the primary driver of cost advantage: BYD received US$3.7 billion in subsidies 2018–2022 (US$2.2 billion in 2022 alone), but subsidies account for only ~5% of BYD's cost advantage over Tesla; vertical integration, scale, and lower-cost talent account for the rest.
- Key risks: margin compression from China price war (gross margin fell from 21.4% in 2023 to 10–15% by Q2 2025); geopolitical tariff barriers (EU +17% on top of 10% base; US 100% tariff); overcapacity risk from locked-in fixed assets; FinDreams battery sales to third parties constrained by conflict-of-interest perception.
- BYD's response to tariffs: ~$1B Turkey plant (EU tariff-free via customs union), plants in Thailand, Hungary, Uzbekistan, Brazil; exports targeted at 20% of total sales in 2025 (up from 10%).

## Claims

- BYD was founded on 10 February 1995 by [[wang-chuanfu]] as Shenzhen BYD Battery Company Limited with CNY 2.5 million capital and 20 employees. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- BYD entered automotive manufacturing in 2003 via acquisition of Xi'an Qinchuan Automobile Company, and built its automotive supply chain by reverse engineering competitor products and supplier parts. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- In 2022, BYD ceased production of all pure ICE vehicles, becoming the first major automaker to do so. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- IGBTs account for 7–10% of total EV manufacturing cost — the second most expensive component after batteries — and BYD produces them in-house via [[byd-semiconductor]] (formally established 2020). [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- BYD holds 1,117 battery patents vs. Tesla's 97, reflecting fundamentally different R&D intensity in the battery domain. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- Through FinDreams Battery, BYD was the world's second-largest EV battery producer in 2024 with 17% market share, behind CATL. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- BYD's battery costs reached as low as RMB 0.4/Wh (USD $0.055/Wh), enabling a 15% cost advantage over Tesla in EV production. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- BYD's in-house chip capability during the 2021–2022 global chip shortage allowed it to increase production while Ford and GM halted lines. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- China's JV mandatory structure (1994–2022) required foreign automakers to limit ownership to ≤50% and partner with Chinese firms; most SOEs with JV ties remain dependent on JV profits. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- BYD's gross profit surged ~500% from CNY 21 billion (2019) to CNY 151 billion (2024), a five-year compounding driven by scale and vertical integration leverage. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
- Wang Chuanfu stated: "a fast decision-making mechanism is one of BYD's important advantages" — BYD's core decision layer remains the founding team. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

## Notes

Research purpose: Hausarbeit (academic paper) comparing four governance/financing models in the Chinese automotive industry. BYD represents the "vertically integrated private" model. The central question is: which governance and financing structure is most likely to create long-term shareholder value in China's automotive industry?

Conflict note on gross margin: [[17-price-war-margin-compression]] reports gross margin declining to 10–15% in Q2 2025, while [[06-financial-performance-shareholder-value]] reports 19.4% for full-year 2024. These are consistent — full-year 2024 peaked, and 2025 saw compression from price war escalation.

The Berkshire exit (Sept 2025) at 3,890% return is notable as a validation data point: a 17-year patient capital investment returned 20x+, suggesting vertical integration combined with founder-led governance can generate substantial long-run shareholder value under the right conditions.
