# SAIC Motor — Academic & Industry Sources (Web Research, May 2026)

**21-query parallel web research identifying peer-reviewed academic papers, primary official sources, and industry reports relevant to SAIC Motor's JV-centric SOE model and EV-era viability.**

## Key takeaways

- Three confirmed ABS 4* / VHB A+ papers directly address the quid-pro-quo JV policy: Bai et al. (AER 2025), Holmes et al. (RES 2015), and Inkpen & Beamish (AMR 1997)
- The "backfired" counter-thesis is covered by Howell (Research Policy 2018): JV rents suppressed domestic innovation via a cannibalization effect
- SAIC's 2023 Annual Report (official PDF confirmed) shows $104.97bn revenue, 52% self-brand / 48% JV sales split — contrast with 2024's sharp decline
- EU Implementing Regulation 2024/2754: SAIC's final anti-subsidy duty is **35.3%** (not 36.3% as provisional); MG EU BEV share collapsed to 2.4% by August 2024
- China's 50/50 JV requirement was formally removed effective 1 January 2022 (NDRC/MOFCOM December 2021)
- NEV market share in China: 41% in 2024 (CAAM); BEVs projected 70–85% share by 2040 (Roland Berger)
- SAIC-VW JV extended to 2040 with China-specific EV platform (locally developed Compact Main Platform, zonal electric architecture)

## Claims

- **Bai et al. (AER 2025)**: Knowledge spillovers from JV affiliation contributed 8.3% of quality improvement in affiliated domestic OEM models vs. non-affiliated, 2001–2014; mediated via worker flows and supplier networks. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Holmes, McGrattan, Prescott (RES 2015)**: Quid-pro-quo policy generates large welfare gains for China and welfare losses for FDI partners. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Howell (Research Policy 2018)**: JV rents created a cannibalization effect that *reduced* innovation incentives in Chinese domestic OEMs with JV partners (1999–2013 data). [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Inkpen & Beamish (AMR 1997)**: JV instability results from shifts in partner bargaining power when one party acquires sufficient knowledge to eliminate partner dependency. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Chan (Competition & Change 2022)**: China's SOEs operate under "managed competition" — the state coordinates competition among subsidiaries via personnel allocation while preserving a field of capable competitors. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Wang (Cornell ILJ 2014)**: CCP's political logic dominates SOE corporate governance; Anglo-Saxon governance forms adopted externally but party oversight constrains effectiveness. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Wang & Tan (Vanderbilt JTIL 2020)**: Xi-era SOE reform shows "retreat of the state" alongside "advance of the CCP"; private shareholders improve performance in survival-driven SOEs but not policy-driven ones. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Thun (Cambridge UP, 2006)**: FDI and local government dynamics shaped automotive JV outcomes differently across Chinese cities; Shanghai-VW and Shanghai-GM are direct case studies. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **SAIC 2023 Annual Report**: Revenue $104.97bn USD; domestic sales 25.184m (YoY +6%); auto exports 4.91m (YoY +58%); 52% self-brand / 48% JV sales split. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **EU Reg. 2024/2754**: Final SAIC countervailing duty = 35.3% (plus 10% MFN = 45.3% total); BYD 17.0%, Geely 18.8%. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **NDRC/MOFCOM 2021**: China removed the 50% foreign equity cap for passenger car manufacturing, effective 1 January 2022. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **McKinsey 2024**: ~50% of vehicles sold in China in 2024 were EVs; consumer preferences rapidly shifting to electrification and "smartification". [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Roland Berger 2024**: BEVs passed 50% mark in China in July 2024; projected 70–85% share by 2040. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **CAAM / CnEVPost 2025**: China NEV market share 41% in 2024 (+9pp); production 12.9m units (+34.4%); BYD #1 at 34.1% NEV share; SAIC and Chery joint-largest exporters. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **Rhodium Group 2024**: Chinese ZEV firms invested $16bn overseas in 2024, surpassing $15bn domestic investment for the first time. [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
- **WardsAuto**: SAIC-VW extended to 2040; new locally-developed Compact Main Platform uses zonal electric architecture (first time in VW Group history). [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]]
