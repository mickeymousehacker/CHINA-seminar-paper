## Result 6 — PRIORITY 2

- **Title**: China NEV Sales December 2024 — CAAM Data
- **Publisher**: CnEVPost (reporting CAAM data)
- **Date**: January 13, 2025
- **URL**: https://cnevpost.com/2025/01/13/china-nev-sales-dec-2024-caam/
- **Primary source**: China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) — https://en.caam.org.cn/
- **Priority**: Priorität 2

**Content** (verbatim from search results):

China NEV sales at record 1.596 million in December 2024, CAAM data show. China produced 31.3 million vehicles in 2024 while the total new vehicle market increased by 4.5% to 31.44 million vehicles. The production of new energy vehicles (NEV) increased by 34.4% to nearly 12.9 million vehicles in 2024. NEV had a market share of 41% in 2024, an increase of 9 percentage points. Battery-electric vehicles (BEV) had a 60% share of the NEV market while the share of Plug-in Hybrids increased to 40%.

BYD topped the China NEV market in 2024 with 34.1% market share, delivering 4.27 million vehicles. Tesla in third place with 6.0%. The largest car exporters from China in 2024 were Chery and SAIC.

**Relevance summary**: Official CAAM production/sales data for 2024 — the definitive market-share numbers. NEV at 41% of total market and SAIC's export leadership (alongside Chery) provide key factual anchors.

## Result 8 — PRIORITY 3

- **Title**: China's Automotive Odyssey: From Joint Ventures to Global EV Dominance
- **Publisher**: IMD (International Institute for Management Development)
- **URL**: https://www.imd.org/ibyimd/asian-hub/chinas-automotive-odyssey-from-joint-ventures-to-global-ev-dominance/
- **Priority**: Priorität 3

**Content** (verbatim from search results):

China's automotive story spans decades of strategic partnerships, policy interventions, and technological leaps. Beginning with early joint ventures in the 1980s — between SAIC and Volkswagen in 1984, and later with General Motors in 1997 — China leveraged foreign expertise to build a domestic automotive industry. These JVs transferred technology, manufacturing know-how, and management practices while providing foreign automakers access to China's vast market. Now, Chinese automakers are moving beyond the JV model to develop indigenous EV technology.

**Relevance summary**: Accessible narrative overview of the JV-to-EV transition story — useful for introductory framing but not citable as primary research.
