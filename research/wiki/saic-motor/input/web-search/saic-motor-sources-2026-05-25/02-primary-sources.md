## Result 3 — PRIORITY 1

- **Title**: EU Implementing Regulation 2024/2754 — Definitive Countervailing Duties on Imports of BEVs from China
- **Publisher**: European Commission / Official Journal of the European Union
- **Date**: Effective 30 October 2024
- **Access URL (EC Press)**: https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_24_5589
- **Access URL (EC Trade)**: https://trade.ec.europa.eu/access-to-markets/en/news/eu-commission-imposes-countervailing-duties-imports-battery-electric-vehicles-bevs-china
- **Priority**: Priorität 1

**Content** (verbatim from search results):

On October 4, 2024, the European Commission proposal to impose definitive countervailing duties of up to 35.3% on imports of battery electric vehicles (BEVs) from China was adopted by the Council. Definitive countervailing duties on imports of battery electric vehicles (BEVs) from China have been adopted by the EU Commission under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2754, applicable as of 30 October 2024.

The tariff rates for SAIC changed during the investigation process:
- Provisional duties (July 2024): SAIC: 37.6%
- Revised provisional duties (August 2024): SAIC: 36.3%
- Final definitive duties: SAIC Group: 35.3%

The Commission employed a sampling methodology, selecting three major Chinese groups — BYD, Geely, and SAIC — to determine representative duty rates. Other cooperating companies received a weighted average rate, while non-cooperating entities were hit with the maximum penalty.

SAIC Motor announced its intention to file a lawsuit with the Court of Justice of the European Union to protect its rights and interests.

In the first half of 2025, while BEV registrations plummeted, MG saw a surge in registrations for the MG HS (PHEV) and the MG3 Hybrid.

By August 2024, MG's share of the EU BEV market had collapsed to 2.4%, down from 4.1% a year prior.

**Relevance summary**: Critical primary regulatory document quantifying the EU's determination that SAIC (the most heavily tariffed of the three sampled OEMs at 35.3%) received illegal subsidies. This is direct evidence for the Financing dimension (state subsidies) and Long-term Viability (export market shock). The 35.3% rate vs. BYD's 17.0% and Geely's 18.8% indicates SAIC was found to be the most heavily subsidized.

## Result 4 — PRIORITY 2

- **Title**: China Negative List for Foreign Direct Investment — Automotive Sector (2021/2022 Amendment removing 50% JV cap)
- **Publishers**: NDRC (National Development and Reform Commission) & MOFCOM (Ministry of Commerce)
- **Date**: Released 27 December 2021; effective 1 January 2022
- **URL**: https://investmentpolicy.unctad.org/investment-policy-monitor/measures/3792/issued-new-negative-list-for-foreign-direct-investment
- **Secondary URL (MOFCOM FDI law text)**: https://fdi.mofcom.gov.cn/EN/come-falvfagui-con.html?id=10691
- **Priority**: Priorität 2

**Content** (verbatim from search results):

China removed restrictions on overseas investment in domestic passenger car manufacturing effective January 1, 2022, through a document released by China's NDRC and Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM) on December 27, 2021.

Under the new policy, foreign companies are no longer subject to a 50% investment cap on the manufacture of all complete passenger vehicles, including internal combustion engine vehicles, and they are no longer required to cap the number of joint ventures in China for the same production line at two.

Since 1994, China had required foreign companies to form a joint venture (JV) with local enterprises to access the domestic passenger car industry, with foreign companies initially able to hold up to 50% equity in the JV before the maximum ownership was loosened to 70% in 2018.

MOFCOM and NDRC jointly published the 2022 version of the Encouraged Industries Catalogue, which became effective on 1 January 2023. Compared with the previous 2020 version, the national sub-catalogue added or expanded items to attract foreign investment into advanced manufacturing industries and modern service industries.

**Relevance summary**: The 2022 removal of the 50/50 JV requirement is the single most important regulatory event for SAIC's viability analysis — it eliminates the legal foundation of the quid-pro-quo policy that built SAIC's business. This is essential for the Long-term Viability dimension.

- **Publisher**: MOFCOM
- **URL**: https://fdi.mofcom.gov.cn/EN/come-falvfagui-con.html?id=10691
- **Priority**: Priorität 2

**Content** (verbatim from search results):

The Foreign Investment Law provides the general legal framework governing foreign investment in China, including FDI rules applicable to automotive joint ventures. The 2019 Foreign Investment Law replaced the Sino-Foreign Equity Joint Venture Law (1979), the Cooperative Joint Venture Law, and the Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise Law, consolidating foreign investment governance.
