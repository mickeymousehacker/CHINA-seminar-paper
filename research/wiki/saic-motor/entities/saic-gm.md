# SAIC-GM (Shanghai General Motors)

**50/50 joint venture between SAIC Motor and General Motors, founded 1997 — once SAIC's most profitable JV, now a major financial liability after a catastrophic 2024.**

## Overview

SAIC-GM was established in 1997 as a 50/50 joint venture between [[saic-motor]] and [[general-motors]], producing Buick, Chevrolet, and Cadillac branded vehicles for the Chinese market. For over two decades it was among the most profitable automotive JVs in China, generating billions of dollars in annual profits shared equally between partners.

The JV followed the classic [[quid-pro-quo-policy]] logic: GM gained market access to China's fast-growing consumer market; SAIC gained technology transfer, management expertise, and a share of JV profits without bearing the full capital risk of product development.

By 2024, SAIC-GM had become SAIC Motor's single greatest financial problem. Vehicle sales collapsed 56.54% year-on-year to 435,007 units. In Q4 2024, SAIC-GM made a massive asset impairment provision that drove SAIC Motor's consolidated Q4 net profit to RMB −5.24 billion and pushed the full-year recurring net profit to RMB −5.409 billion. ## Key facts

- **Founded**: 1997
- **Ownership**: 50/50 — [[saic-motor]] and [[general-motors]]
- **Brands**: Buick, Chevrolet, Cadillac
- **Sales 2024**: 435,007 units (−56.54% YoY) [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
- **Production 2024**: 421,725 units [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
- **Key event 2024**: Large asset impairment provision in Q4 2024; SAIC bears 50% of the write-down as equity-method investee loss [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
- **Impact on SAIC**: Investment income fell 52% YoY; primary driver of 88.19% net profit decline [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]

## Structural context

SAIC-GM's decline reflects the broader challenge facing legacy ICE-focused JVs in China's EV transition. GM's Chinese lineup was heavily weighted toward gasoline-powered sedans and SUVs — segments losing share rapidly to domestic EV-native brands like [[byd-company]] and newer entrants. Unlike [[saic-volkswagen]] (which signed a 10-year extension to 2040), there is no public announcement of an SAIC-GM JV extension, suggesting strategic uncertainty.
