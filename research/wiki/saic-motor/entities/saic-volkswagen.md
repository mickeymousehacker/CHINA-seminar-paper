# SAIC Volkswagen

**50/50 joint venture between SAIC Motor and Volkswagen Group, China's longest-running major automotive JV (founded 1984), extended to 2040 despite declining sales.**

## Overview

SAIC Volkswagen (SAIC-VW) was established in 1984 as a 50/50 joint venture between [[saic-motor]] and [[volkswagen-group]], producing Volkswagen and Škoda branded vehicles in China. It is one of the oldest and historically largest automotive JVs in China, predating the formal codification of the [[quid-pro-quo-policy]] and serving as a model for subsequent JV approvals.

On its 40th anniversary in 2024, SAIC-VW signed a 10-year extension to 2040 — a significant strategic commitment by both parties to the JV model even as the broader market shifts toward EVs. The extension is accompanied by plans to launch 18 new models by 2030 and the introduction of a new AUDI brand manufactured under the JV umbrella. [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]

Despite the extension, SAIC-VW faces meaningful headwinds: sales declined 5.51% in 2024. The JV's ICE-heavy portfolio is losing ground to domestic EV-native competitors in mid-market segments, where Volkswagen's brand premium is eroding. [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]

## Key facts

- **Founded**: 1984 (first major Sino-foreign automotive JV)
- **Ownership**: 50/50 — [[saic-motor]] and [[volkswagen-group]]
- **Brands**: Volkswagen, Škoda, AUDI (new)
- **JV extended to**: 2040 (signed 2024 on 40th anniversary) [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
- **Sales 2024**: 1,148,091 units (−5.51% YoY) [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
- **Production 2024**: 1,147,257 units [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
- **New models planned**: 18 new models by 2030 [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]

## Strategic significance

SAIC-VW remains [[saic-motor]]'s largest single production subsidiary by volume (1.148M units vs. SAIC-GM's 435K). Its extension to 2040 signals that the JV model is not being abandoned, but the commitment also locks capital into a partnership that relies heavily on the competitive viability of VW's ICE and hybrid models in China through the mid-2030s.

The new AUDI brand under SAIC-VW is notable: it represents an attempt to re-enter the premium segment under a differentiated brand identity, possibly with more local (electrified) product development authority. [[2026-05-23-saic-annual-report-2024]]
