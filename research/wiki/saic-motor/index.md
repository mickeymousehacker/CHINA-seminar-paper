- [[2026-05-23-saic-motor-financials-2020-2024]] — SAIC Motor 5-year financial time series 2020–2024 (web research); net profit trend, EPS, sales volumes. Data flags resolved by Annual Report.
- [[2026-05-25-saic-motor-sources-web-research]] — 21-query parallel web research identifying peer-reviewed academic papers, primary official sources, and industry reports; includes confirmed ABS 4* papers on quid-pro-quo policy and JV instability theory.
- [[2026-05-27-holmes-mcgrattan-prescott-2015-quid-pro-quo-res]] — Holmes, McGrattan & Prescott (2015), Review of Economic Studies; multicountry GE model quantifying QPQ welfare effects: China +5%, advanced economies −0.3–0.5%; patent microdata confirming China-only property rights transfer.
- [[2026-05-27-howell-2018-jv-technology-adoption-backfired]] — Howell (2018), Research Policy (ABS 3); JV rents created a cannibalization effect that suppressed domestic innovation; firms with JVs reduced quality/price under fuel economy standards; exports dominated by non-JV firms; "like opium" quote.
- [[2026-05-27-fan-wong-zhang-2007-politically-connected-ceos-jfe]] — Fan, Wong & Zhang (2007), Journal of Financial Economics (ABS 4*); 27% of Chinese IPO-firm CEOs are politically connected; these firms underperform by ~18% in 3-year post-IPO returns; boards loaded with bureaucrats, not professionals; "grabbing hand" mechanism.
- [[2026-05-27-jia-huang-zhang-2019-public-governance-soe-innovation-amj]] — Jia, Huang & Zhang (2019), Academy of Management Journal (ABS 4*); SOEs under weak governance respond to China's innovation mandate by producing more patents but fewer novel ones; quantity-over-novelty bias explained by multitasking moral hazard; board alignment + public governance quality are key moderators.
- [[2026-05-27-bortolotti-faccio-2009-government-control-privatized-firms-rfs]] — Bortolotti & Faccio (2009), Review of Financial Studies (ABS 4*); 62.4% of OECD "privatized" firms remain under government control at end-2000 via ownership pyramids or golden shares; civil-law systems use pyramids (SAIC's pattern), common-law systems use golden shares; political economy, not firm quality, drives government retention.
- [[2026-05-27-megginson-netter-2001-from-state-to-market-jel]] — Megginson & Netter (2001), Journal of Economic Literature (ABS 4*); authoritative survey of 100+ privatization studies: privatized firms show +3.96pp profitability, +19.14% efficiency, +83.21% output across 211 firms; SOEs underperform because of soft budget constraints and multiple objectives; Chinese SOEs noted as especially constrained by social welfare burdens.
- [[2026-05-27-shleifer-vishny-1994-politicians-and-firms-qje]] — Shleifer & Vishny (1994), Quarterly Journal of Economics (ABS 4*); foundational "grabbing hand" theory: politicians control firms to maximize patronage employment (L) rather than firm value; control rights — not cash flow rights — determine whether privatization improves efficiency; politicians actively resist strong manager equity incentives.
- [[2026-05-27-bruton-peng-et-al-2015-soe-hybrid-organizations-amp]] — Bruton, Peng et al. (2015), Academy of Management Perspectives (ABS 3); 36-case study across 23 countries showing SOEs as hybrid organizations on a 3×3 ownership×control matrix; ownership and control are separable; SASAC explicitly identified as Chinese high-control instrument (p.100); soft budget constraints cause firms to "ignore market signals and many key technologies, particularly during periods of technological ferment" (p.102); CEO government background drives employment/social goals over profit (pp.103–104).

- [[shanghai-sasac]] — Shanghai municipal SASAC; ultimate controller of SAIC Motor via SAIC Group (63.27%).

## Concepts

- [[joint-venture-model]] — China's 50/50 JV arrangement for foreign OEM market access; SAIC's defining strategic model.
- [[quid-pro-quo-policy]] — Market access for technology transfer policy; created SAIC's JV opportunities; formally removed 2022.
- [[state-owned-enterprise]] — Governance archetype; SAIC controlled by Shanghai SASAC; "managed competition" and CCP political logic.
- [[jv-instability]] — IB theory (Inkpen & Beamish AMR 1997) on how knowledge-parity shifts erode JV rationale; applies to SAIC's current position (→ global concepts wiki).
