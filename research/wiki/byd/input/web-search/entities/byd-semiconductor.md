# BYD Semiconductor

**BYD's in-house automotive-grade power semiconductor unit, formally established in 2020, producing IGBTs and SiC chips that are the second most expensive component in an EV after the battery.**

## Overview

BYD Semiconductor was formally established in 2020 as a spin-out of [[byd-company]]'s existing microelectronics division. It produces automotive-grade power semiconductors including Insulated-Gate Bipolar Transistors (IGBTs) and Silicon Carbide (SiC) chips — the core power electronics that control electricity flow from battery to motor. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

[[igbt-semiconductor|IGBTs]] account for 7–10% of total EV manufacturing cost, making them the second most expensive component after the battery. By developing and manufacturing these chips in-house, BYD eliminates a key external single-point-of-failure in its supply chain. This proved decisive during the global chip shortage of 2021–2022: while Ford and General Motors halted production lines, BYD maintained and even increased production output. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

BYD's overall in-house component procurement rate stands at approximately 70–75%, with semiconductors as one of the strategically critical internally supplied components. The Nomad Semiconductor "BYD Semiconductor Deep Dive" provides detailed analysis of the product portfolio and competitive positioning within the automotive power semiconductor market. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
