## Result 1
- **Title**: BYD Company - Wikipedia
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BYD_Company
- **Content**:

BYD was founded by Wang Chuanfu in February 1995 as a battery manufacturing company. Wang Chuanfu gathered a team of 20 people and the company was formally founded on 10 February 1995 as Shenzhen BYD Battery Company Limited with CN¥ 2.5 million of capital, with a focus on producing rechargeable nickel–cadmium (NiCd) batteries.

Wang, at 29 years old, founded the company after noticing an opportunity presented by the shift in Japanese companies from NiCd to high-value nickel–metal hydride (NiMH) and lithium-ion (Li-ion) batteries while he was working as a vice supervisor at the Beijing Nonferrous Research Institute.

In 1996, BYD began manufacturing lithium-ion batteries, coinciding with the growth of mobile phones, and went on to supply its batteries to Motorola and Nokia in 2000 and 2002, respectively.

BYD is characterized by its vertical supply chain system, originating as a battery company in 1995 before venturing into cars (via BYD Auto) in 2003. When BYD entered the automobile industry, it built a fully integrated automotive supply chain by reverse engineering competitor products and supplier parts, and the company had achieved proficiency in producing key components for electric vehicles, such as batteries, motors, and electronic control.

BYD Auto, established in 2003, has since become the world's largest manufacturer of plug-in electric vehicles, and since 2009, BYD's automotive business has accounted for over 50% of its revenue, surpassing 80% by 2023.

## Result 3
- **Title**: How BYD grew from a phone battery maker to EV giant taking on Tesla
- **URL**: https://www.cnbc.com/2024/01/05/how-byd-grew-from-a-phone-battery-maker-to-ev-giant-taking-on-tesla.html
- **Content**:

BYD's transformation timeline:
- 1995: Founded as Shenzhen BYD Battery Company Limited (NiCd batteries, CN¥ 2.5 million capital, 20 employees)
- 1996: Began manufacturing lithium-ion batteries
- 2000: First supply contract with Motorola
- 2002: Nokia supply contract; listed on Hong Kong Stock Exchange
- 2003: Acquired Xi'an Qinchuan Automobile Company to enter automotive manufacturing
- 2008: Berkshire Hathaway investment ($230M for ~10% stake)
- 2010: First plug-in hybrid EV (BYD F3DM)
- 2020: Blade Battery announced
- 2022: Ceased production of pure ICE vehicles
- 2024: Surpassed Tesla in global EV sales
