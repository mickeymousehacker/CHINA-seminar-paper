## Result 1
- **Title**: BYD Blade Battery: Leading a New Era in Lithium Iron Phosphate Batteries
- **URL**: https://batteryswapstation.com/what-is-byd-blade-battery/
- **Content**:

BYD's Blade Battery uses LFP chemistry combined with a CTP (cell-to-pack) design. LFP uses iron and phosphate instead of nickel and cobalt, making batteries safer, longer-lasting, and less expensive to produce, at the price of lower energy density.

**Safety:** The chemical structure of lithium iron phosphate provides inherently greater thermal and chemical stability compared to nickel-based chemistries. BYD has demonstrated through extreme testing that the Blade battery emits no smoke or fire after being penetrated in nail tests, with surface temperatures reaching only 30 to 60°C.

**Cost Efficiency:** BYD's LFP Blade batteries benefit from lower production costs since they use abundant, less expensive materials like iron and phosphate and don't use costly cobalt. The blade battery's simplified structure reduces secondary components by 40%.

**Longevity:** BYD says the Blade Battery can handle over 5,000 charge and discharge cycles before any major degradation happens, which reduces the need for battery replacement, lowering total ownership cost.
