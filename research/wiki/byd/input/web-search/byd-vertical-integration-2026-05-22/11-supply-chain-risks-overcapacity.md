- **URL**: https://www.ainvest.com/news/byd-path-ev-supremacy-vertical-integration-innovation-secure-dominance-2506/
- **Content**:

BYD produces its own lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries through subsidiaries like FinDreams, with control extending to raw materials including lithium mines in Brazil and vertically integrating refining, cathode production, and cell assembly. By manufacturing approximately 75% of its components in-house, including batteries and semiconductors, BYD has achieved unmatched cost efficiency and supply chain control.

BYD achieves a 15% cost advantage over Tesla in EV production, with battery costs as low as RMB 0.4/Wh (USD $0.055/Wh) for its latest models. BYD's vertically integrated model spanning battery production, semiconductor design, and raw material sourcing allows it to absorb margin pressures that would cripple competitors dependent on third-party suppliers.

## Result 2
- **Title**: BYD and Vertical Integration
- **URL**: https://smitka.substack.com/p/byd-and-vertical-integration
- **Content**:

A challenge for vertically integrated companies is the potential for overcapacity, where if market demand shifts or technology evolves, companies may find themselves locked into expensive, underutilized assets. For instance, a manufacturer investing heavily in nickel-based cathode production could face losses if the industry shifts toward lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) chemistries.

Some analysts question whether BYD's forward integration into cars makes sense, as its battery subsidiary arrangement may not instill confidence in other car companies and impedes their battery sales to external customers.
