## Result 1
- **Title**: BYD's strategy to overtake Tesla in EV production globally
- **URL**: https://www.automotivemanufacturingsolutions.com/electrification/how-chinas-byd-surpassed-tesla-with-production-and-battery-tech-reshaping-the-global-ev-market/304649
- **Content**:

Both companies pursue vertical integration to reduce costs, but their focuses differ. Tesla independently develops self-driving algorithms, chips, batteries, electric motors, vehicle bodies, and electronic electric architectures. BYD's vertical integration focuses on cost leadership, making full use of the advantage of relatively low labor costs by employing more people along with cheaper equipment to transform the production process and produce high-cost auto parts themselves.

## Result 2
- **Title**: BYD Company Limited and Tesla: vertical integration AB side
- **URL**: https://news.futunn.com/en/post/30011957/byd-company-limited-and-tesla-vertical-integration-ab-side
- **Content**:

BYD produces nearly 80% of its core components in-house, more than double that of Tesla. Rather than relying on external suppliers for core components, BYD manufactures batteries, power electronics, electric motors, and semiconductors in-house.

Unlike Tesla's premium positioning, BYD excels through vertical integration in battery manufacturing, controlling lithium iron phosphate (LFP) and blade battery production, which enables aggressive pricing strategies with vehicles starting as low as $10,000-$15,000 in Chinese markets.

Industry estimates suggest this vertical integration enables 30–40% lower battery costs versus competitors relying on external suppliers — a structural cost advantage that directly enables BYD's aggressive vehicle pricing strategy.

BYD's gross profit margin stands at 19.4%, outperforming Tesla's 17.9%, with this margin superiority underscoring BYD's operational advantage derived from its vertically integrated model.
