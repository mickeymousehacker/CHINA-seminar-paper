# IGBT Semiconductor

**Insulated-Gate Bipolar Transistors (IGBTs) and Silicon Carbide (SiC) chips are the power electronics that control electricity flow from battery to motor in an EV — the second most expensive component after the battery (7–10% of total manufacturing cost), and a strategic in-house production target for [[byd-company]].**

## Summary

IGBTs are high-power semiconductor switches that govern the energy conversion and motor control functions in an electric vehicle's powertrain. Along with SiC (silicon carbide) devices — which offer higher switching frequency and thermal efficiency at the cost of higher production complexity — they form the core of a vehicle's power electronics system. In EVs, these components must handle high voltages and currents with millisecond-level precision, making them safety-critical and precision-engineered. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

At 7–10% of total EV manufacturing cost, IGBTs are the second most expensive EV component after the battery. The global automotive IGBT market was historically dominated by European and Japanese suppliers (Infineon, ON Semiconductor, Mitsubishi Electric). [[byd-company]]'s decision to develop and manufacture IGBTs in-house via [[byd-semiconductor]] removed a key external dependency and created a structural cost and supply advantage. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

The competitive significance was demonstrated during the 2021–2022 global chip shortage: while Ford, General Motors, and other automakers dependent on external chip suppliers halted or reduced production, BYD maintained and increased production because its internal IGBT and chip supply was not subject to spot market allocation. This single episode arguably accelerated BYD's market share gains by 12–18 months vs. what organic demand growth alone would have achieved. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
