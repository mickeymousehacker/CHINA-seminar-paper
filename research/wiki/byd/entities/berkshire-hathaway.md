# Berkshire Hathaway

**Warren Buffett's holding company; invested $230 million in [[byd-company]] in 2008 for a ~10% stake and fully exited in September 2025 at a 3,890% return (20x+).**

## Overview

Berkshire Hathaway, led by Warren Buffett and the late Charlie Munger, invested approximately $230 million in BYD in September 2008, acquiring about 225 million shares representing ~10% of the company. The deal was largely championed by Charlie Munger, who later said: "I have never helped do anything at Berkshire that was as good as BYD." The investment coincided with a period when [[byd-company]] had net debt of CNY 4.8 billion and was still primarily a consumer battery and ICE automobile company with nascent EV ambitions. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

Berkshire began selling its BYD shares in 2022, after BYD's share price had risen more than twentyfold. The full exit was completed in September 2025. Over the 17-year holding period, the investment grew over 20-fold, generating a 3,890% return — one of the highest absolute returns from a single equity investment in Berkshire's history. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

The Berkshire investment served a dual function for [[byd-company]]: it provided validation by the world's most respected value investor, signaling to global capital markets that BYD's technology and business model merited confidence, and it provided capital that BYD used to supplement working capital, repay debt, and fund R&D expansion during a critical growth phase. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
