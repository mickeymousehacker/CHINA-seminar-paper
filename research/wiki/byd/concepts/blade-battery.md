# Blade Battery

**BYD's proprietary lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery using a cell-to-pack (CTP) design where long, flat "blade" cells are arrayed directly into the pack structure — eliminating modules, reducing components by 40%, and achieving industry-leading safety at competitive energy density.**

## Summary

The Blade Battery, announced by [[byd-company]] in 2020, is the technological centerpiece of BYD's [[vertical-integration]] strategy in the battery domain. It combines [[lfp-chemistry]] (lithium iron phosphate, iron and phosphate instead of nickel and cobalt) with a cell-to-pack (CTP) architecture in which blade-shaped cells are arrayed directly into the structural battery pack, eliminating the intermediate module layer present in conventional battery designs. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

**Safety:** LFP chemistry's inherent thermal stability is reinforced by the CTP design. BYD's nail penetration tests show zero smoke or fire, with surface temperatures reaching only 30–60°C — a dramatic contrast to nickel-based chemistries that can enter thermal runaway. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

**Cost:** Elimination of cobalt removes the most price-volatile and geopolitically concentrated element from the bill of materials. The simplified structure (40% fewer secondary components) lowers manufacturing complexity and cost. Battery costs reached as low as RMB 0.4/Wh ($0.055/Wh). [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

**Longevity:** >5,000 charge/discharge cycles before major degradation, significantly lowering total cost of ownership and reducing replacement battery demand. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

**Energy density:** The blade geometry, while using lower-energy-density LFP chemistry, achieves 439–450 Wh/L volumetric density, enabling >600 km range. This addresses the classic LFP weakness versus nickel-based chemistries without changing the underlying chemistry. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

A second-generation Blade Battery is expected in August 2026 with 190 Wh/kg energy density and projected range exceeding 1,000 km. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]

The Blade Battery is produced by [[findreams]] Battery (BYD's battery subsidiary) and is also sold to third-party OEMs — BorgWarner signed an 8-year supply agreement with FinDreams Battery for Blade Battery cells. [[2026-05-22-byd-vertical-integration-web-research]]
