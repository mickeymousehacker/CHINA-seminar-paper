# 📖 ABSCHNITT 2.1 — GOVERNANCE (~1,3 Seiten)

## 2.1.1 Eigentumsstruktur — der erste Absatz

**Argumentkern:** Zweistufige Staatskontrolle via SASAC-Pyramide.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2025, p. 69; AR 2024 Governance-Sektion.

**Wichtigste Zahlen:**
- SAIC Group (staatliche juristische Person): **63,71%** der Aktien (AR 2025, p. 69)
- Weitere staatliche Anteilseigner: China COSCO Shipping 5,91%; Yuejin Motor Group 3,60%; Central Huijin Investment 0,86%
- Total Tier-1 + Tier-2 staatlicher Anteil: **~73,5%**
- Börsenplatzierung: Shanghai Stock Exchange (Code 600104), A-Aktien

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ (verwendbar als Starttext):**

> SAIC Motor Corporation Limited operates under a two-tier state ownership structure. Shanghai Automotive Industry (Group) Co., Ltd. — itself wholly controlled by the Shanghai State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration Commission (Shanghai SASAC) — holds 63.71% of SAIC Motor's listed shares (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 69). Combined with additional state-owned blockholders including China COSCO Shipping (5.91%) and Yuejin Motor Group (3.60%), the cumulative state share approaches 73.5%. While SAIC Motor is publicly listed on the Shanghai Stock Exchange (600104), control rights — board appointments, strategic approvals, and capital allocation — remain with SASAC. This ownership pyramid is the canonical civil-law mechanism for retaining government control over nominally privatized firms (Bortolotti & Faccio, 2009, p. 2908).

**Alternative Formulierung (kürzer):**

> The ownership structure of SAIC Motor reflects a textbook case of partial privatization with retained state control. Shanghai SASAC controls SAIC Group, which in turn holds 63.71% of the listed company (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 69); voting blocks held by additional state entities push the effective state stake above 70%. The Shanghai Stock Exchange listing (code 600104) provides market discipline only at the margins, since the controlling shareholder is non-transferable.

## 2.1.3 Akademischer Anker 1 — Shleifer & Vishny (1994)

**Argumentkern:** Politische Kontrolle ≠ Cash-Flow-Kontrolle. Politische Kontrolleure (SASAC) maximieren Beschäftigung (L) und politische Stabilität, nicht Firmenwert.

**Quelle:** Shleifer & Vishny (1994), QJE, ABS 4*

**Kernpropositionen mit Seitenzahlen:**
- **Prop. 1** (p. 1005): *"The allocation of resources is independent of the allocation of cash flow rights between the Treasury and private investors"* — Privatisierung ohne Kontrolltransfer ist irrelevant.
- **Prop. 5** (p. 1011): Unter Management-Kontrolle ist L niedriger und Allokation effizienter.
- **Prop. 13** (p. 1020): Politiker bevorzugen Manager mit *niedrigem* Eigenkapitalanteil — weil starke Eigentümer politischen Forderungen besser widerstehen.

**Spezifisches Zitat (p. 1019):**

> *"Politicians want to control firms precisely because such firms have large cash flows from which the Treasury or private shareholders benefit... This is why profitable monopolies are always nationalized or publicly regulated."*

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> The theoretical foundation for evaluating SAIC's governance derives from Shleifer and Vishny's (1994) seminal model of politician-firm interaction. Their central insight — Proposition 1 — is that the allocation of real resources within a firm depends not on the allocation of *cash flow rights* but on the allocation of *control rights* (Shleifer & Vishny, 1994, p. 1005). Privatization that transfers equity stakes without transferring control therefore produces little change in firm behavior. Shleifer and Vishny further demonstrate (Proposition 13, p. 1020) that politicians actively prefer managers with weak equity stakes, because well-incentivized managers are better able to resist political demands. Applied to SAIC, this framework predicts precisely the pattern observed in the annual report: a publicly listed firm with a non-transferable controlling shareholder, a board dominated by political appointees, and management compensation structures that are not aligned with shareholder value maximization.

## 2.1.4 Akademischer Anker 2 — Fan, Wong & Zhang (2007)

**Argumentkern:** Empirische Bestätigung der Shleifer-Vishny-Theorie für *chinesische* teilprivatisierte Firmen — SAICs exakter Fall.

**Quelle:** Fan et al. (2007), JFE, ABS 4*. Stichprobe: 790 IPO-Firmen, 1993–2001.

**Schlüsselzahlen:**
- **27%** der CEOs in teilprivatisierten chinesischen Firmen sind aktuelle/ehemalige Regierungsbeamte (p. 335, Table 1)
- **−18%** kumulierte Aktienrendite über 3 Jahre nach IPO bei politisch verbundenen Firmen (p. 340, Table 3): −7% (J1), −10% (J2), −15% (J3)
- **−21% Umsatzwachstum, −24% Gewinnwachstum** (p. 341, Table 4)
- Boards politisch verbundener Firmen: **36% Bürokraten** vs. 19% bei unverbundenen; 34% Professional Directors vs. 47% (p. 350, Table 10)

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> Fan, Wong and Zhang (2007) provide the strongest empirical test of this framework for the Chinese context. Examining 790 partially privatized Chinese IPO firms over the period 1993–2001, they find that 27% have politically connected CEOs — defined as current or former government officials (p. 335). These firms underperform politically unconnected peers by approximately 18% in three-year post-IPO cumulative abnormal returns (p. 340) and exhibit substantially weaker accounting performance: sales growth lower by 21% and earnings growth lower by 24% (p. 341). Board composition reinforces the pattern: politically connected firms appoint bureaucrats to 36% of directorships compared with 19% in unconnected firms, while reducing the share of directors with professional backgrounds by 13 percentage points (p. 350). The authors interpret these findings as consistent with the "grabbing hand" hypothesis: bureaucratic management extracts resources from listed SOEs to satisfy political objectives at shareholders' expense (p. 353). SAIC's governance structure — SASAC-appointed leadership, state-dominated board composition, and a non-tradable controlling stake — places it squarely in the population for which these effects are documented.

**WICHTIG**: Schreibe nicht "Fan et al. proven this is true for SAIC" — die Stichprobe ist 1993–2001 und enthält SAIC nicht explizit. Schreibe stattdessen: *"SAIC's governance structure places it squarely in the population for which these effects are documented"* oder *"the structural conditions Fan et al. identify are present in SAIC's governance architecture"*.

## 2.1.5 Reform 2025 — die Einschränkung

**Argumentkern:** Auch Reformen ändern die fundamentale Eigentumsstruktur nicht.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2025, pp. 35–36.

**Faktum:** Aufsichtsrat abgeschafft unter neuem Gesellschaftsrecht; Audit Committee übernimmt Kontrollfunktion.

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ (Schlussabsatz von 2.1):**

> In 2025, SAIC formally restructured its governance to comply with China's revised Company Law: the Board of Supervisors was abolished, with its statutory functions transferred to a strengthened Audit Committee of the Board of Directors (SAIC Motor, 2026, pp. 35–36). While this reform represents formal modernization, it does not address the underlying ownership structure or the codified Party leadership mandate. The persistence of the 63.71% controlling SASAC stake, combined with the explicit "two consistencies" principle, means that the structural conditions identified by Shleifer and Vishny (1994) and empirically confirmed by Fan et al. (2007) remain in place. The governance reform of 2025 is consistent with Megginson and Netter's (2001, p. 335) observation that mixed-ownership enterprises are not significantly more profitable than pure SOEs — full control transfer, not procedural modernization, is required to alter the underlying incentive structure.

# 💰 ABSCHNITT 3.1 — FINANCING (~1,3 Seiten)

## 3.1.1 Das historische JV-Modell — Einleitungsabsatz

**Argumentkern:** SAICs Finanzierungslogik war jahrzehntelang kapitalleicht durch JV-Co-Investment. Diese Logik ist 2024 strukturell gebrochen.

**Quellen:** SAIC AR 2024; Holmes et al. (2015) für theoretischen Rahmen.

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> SAIC's historical financing model was structurally capital-light. Under the quid pro quo regulatory regime, Volkswagen (since 1984) and General Motors (since 1997) each contributed 50% of the equity capital and bore half of the operational risk in their joint ventures with SAIC, while SAIC received a corresponding share of JV profits in the form of investment income (Holmes et al., 2015, p. 1161). For more than two decades, this arrangement generated stable, high-margin dividend streams without requiring SAIC to absorb the full cost of vehicle development, plant construction, or distribution build-out. The model was, in effect, an asymmetric capital partnership: the Chinese state supplied market access as its "capital contribution," while foreign OEMs supplied physical capital and technology (Holmes et al., 2015, p. 1158).

---

## 3.1.2 Der strukturelle Bruch 2024

**Argumentkern:** Das kapitalleichte Modell hat eine versteckte Asymmetrie offenbart — SAIC trägt 50% der JV-Verluste, ohne die operative Kontrolle zu haben.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2024, Section II.

**Zahlen 2024 (alle aus AR 2024):**
- Umsatz: RMB 627,59 Mrd. (−15,73% YoY)
- Auto-Manufacturing-Umsatz: RMB 614,07 Mrd. (Bruttomarge 9,38%)
- Nettogewinn (reported): RMB 1,666 Mrd. (−88,19%)
- **Nettogewinn ex Sonderposten: RMB −5,409 Mrd. (Verlust)**
- ROE (gewichtet): **0,58%** (2023: 4,98%; 2022: 5,84%)
- EPS: RMB 0,145 (2023: 1,226 — Fast Nulllinie)
- SAIC-GM Absatz: −56,54% auf 435.007 Einheiten
- Q4-Nettogewinn: RMB **−5,24 Mrd.** (SAIC-GM Impairment dominiert)

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> The structural weakness of the co-investment model became apparent in 2024. As SAIC-GM's annual vehicle sales collapsed by 56.54% to 435,007 units, the joint venture recognized a substantial asset impairment provision in the fourth quarter, of which SAIC absorbed 50% as equity-method investee losses (SAIC Motor, 2025, Section II). The financial consequences were severe: full-year consolidated revenue fell 15.73% to RMB 627.59 billion; reported net profit declined 88.19% to RMB 1.666 billion; and net profit excluding non-recurring items turned negative at RMB −5.41 billion (SAIC Motor, 2025, Section II). The weighted average return on equity collapsed to 0.58%, an order of magnitude below the 4.98% reported for 2023 and the 5.84% reported for 2022. Q4 alone showed a net loss of RMB 5.24 billion. The capital-light model had, in effect, transformed into a capital-trapped model: SAIC remained obligated to absorb half of JV losses but lacked the operational control rights necessary to drive restructuring of the foreign-led joint ventures.

## 3.1.3 Erholung 2025 — mit kritischer Einordnung

**Argumentkern:** Die 2025er Erholung ist überwiegend ein Basiseffekt, kein struktureller Turnaround.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2025, pp. 5–6.

**Zahlen 2025:**
- Umsatz: RMB 646,2 Mrd. (+5,22% YoY)
- Gesamtumsatz inkl. Finance: RMB 656,2 Mrd. (+4,57%)
- Nettogewinn (reported): RMB 10,11 Mrd. **(+506,45%)**
- **Nettogewinn ex Sonderposten: RMB 7,42 Mrd.** (vs. −5,41 Mrd. 2024) — echte operative Verbesserung
- ROE: **3,43%** (+2,85 pct)
- EPS: RMB 0,885 (vs. 0,145)
- Aber: Net profit ex non-recurring 2025 (7,42 Mrd.) bleibt **unter 2023** (10,04 Mrd.)

**⚠️ Wichtigste Frame-Regel:** NICHT "Turnaround" oder "recovery" framen. Bezeichnung: "Basiseffekt", "base-year effect", "absence of one-time impairment".

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> SAIC's reported financial performance recovered substantially in 2025: net profit attributable to shareholders rose to RMB 10.11 billion, an apparent year-on-year increase of 506% (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 5). This headline figure, however, is largely an arithmetic consequence of the absence of the one-time 2024 SAIC-GM impairment rather than an underlying operational improvement. The more informative measure — net profit excluding non-recurring items — improved from RMB −5.41 billion in 2024 to RMB 7.42 billion in 2025 (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 5). While this represents a genuine recovery from operating losses, the figure remains 26% below the comparable 2023 level of RMB 10.04 billion. Weighted average ROE recovered to 3.43%, still significantly below SAIC's 2022 level (5.84%) and an order of magnitude below the levels typical of private Chinese automotive firms during the same period (BYD's 2024 ROE: approximately 22%). The 2025 figures are best interpreted as stabilization rather than structural recovery: the operating loss of 2024 has been arrested, but the underlying capital efficiency of the JV-centric business model remains substantially impaired.

## 3.1.5 Akademischer Anker — Megginson & Netter (2001)

**Schlüsselzitat (p. 335):**
> *"SOEs and mixed (state and private) ownership enterprises are significantly less profitable and productive than are privately owned firms... mixed enterprises are no more profitable than SOEs, suggesting that full private control, not just partial ownership, is essential to achieving performance improvement."*

**Schlüsselzitat (p. 325, China-spezifisch):**
> *"[Chinese] SOEs are burdened with so many social welfare responsibilities suggests that it will be extraordinarily difficult to implement a privatization program large enough to seriously undermine the state's economic role."*

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ (Schlussabsatz Financing):**

> Megginson and Netter's (2001) authoritative survey of approximately 100 empirical privatization studies provides the comparative benchmark against which SAIC's performance must be evaluated. Aggregating results from 211 fully privatized firms across 41 countries, they document a post-privatization profitability improvement of 3.96 percentage points and an efficiency improvement of 19.14% (Megginson & Netter, 2001, pp. 354–356, Table 5). Critically, however, mixed-ownership enterprises — firms partially privatized while remaining under state control, SAIC's structural type — do not capture these gains. As Megginson and Netter note, drawing on Boardman and Vining (1989), "mixed enterprises are no more profitable than SOEs, suggesting that full private control, not just partial ownership, is essential to achieving performance improvement" (p. 335). The Chinese context is explicitly addressed: "[Chinese] SOEs are burdened with so many social welfare responsibilities [that] it will be extraordinarily difficult to implement a privatization program large enough to seriously undermine the state's economic role" (p. 325). SAIC's structure thus places it precisely in the category of firms for which neither continued state ownership nor partial listing produces the efficiency gains documented in the broader privatization literature.

# 🏭 ABSCHNITT 4.1 — CAPABILITY BUILDING (~1,5 Seiten) ⭐ DEIN STÄRKSTES KAPITEL

> **Warum hier anfangen:** Bai et al. (2025) nennt SAIC explizit als Fall — du bist die einzige in der Gruppe, die diesen kausalen Nachweis hat. Wenn dieser Abschnitt sitzt, dominiert der Rest des Papers.

## 4.1.1 Der QPQ-Rahmen — Holmes et al. (2015)

**Argumentkern:** Quid pro quo ist eine Steuer auf Technologiekapital: ausländische Firmen geben Technologie ab, um Marktzugang zu bekommen, halten aber bewusst die fortschrittlichste Technologie zurück.

**Quelle:** Holmes, McGrattan & Prescott (2015), RES, ABS 4*

**Schlüsselzahlen/Zitate:**
- China Wohlfahrtsgewinn: **~5% in Konsumtermen** (1990–2010)
- Fortgeschrittene Volkswirtschaften verlieren **0,3–0,5%** Konsum
- 1994 Automotive Policy: *"capacity for manufacturing products which attain the international technological levels of the 1990s"* (p. 1161)
- Patentrechte China-only: *"Shanghai Bell ownership applies only in China"* (p. 1158)

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ (Eröffnung des Capability-Kapitels):**

> The structural framework that built SAIC's modern manufacturing capability was China's quid pro quo policy, formalized in the 1994 Automotive Industry Development Policy. Under this regime, foreign automakers seeking access to the Chinese market were required to form 50/50 joint ventures with approved domestic partners — invariably SOEs — and to transfer technology "at international levels of the 1990s," subject to a 50% cap on foreign equity (Holmes et al., 2015, p. 1161). Holmes, McGrattan and Prescott (2015) formalize this arrangement as an implicit tax on technology capital: foreign firms are induced to deploy less of their best technology in China because any technology transferred becomes Chinese property within the country (p. 1158). In their multi-country dynamic general equilibrium model, this regime generates an approximately 5% welfare gain for China (in consumption-equivalent terms) over the period 1990–2010, with corresponding losses of 0.3–0.5% for the foreign partner economies (Holmes et al., 2015, Section 6). The policy was, in effect, a structured technology transfer financed by market access rents — an arrangement under which SAIC, as the chosen domestic partner of both Volkswagen (1984) and General Motors (1997), was a primary beneficiary.

## 4.1.2 Bai et al. (2025) — DER HAUPTBEFUND (NUTZE DIES STARK!)

**Argumentkern:** Empirischer kausaler Beleg, dass QPQ-JVs Qualitäts-Spillover an SAIC-Eigenmarken erzeugten. Erstes Paper, das diesen Effekt quantifiziert.

**Quelle:** Bai, Barwick, Cao & Li (2025), AER, ABS 4* — DEIN STÄRKSTES PAPER.

**Schlüsselzitat aus Abstract (p. 1):**
> *"knowledge spillovers due to ownership affiliation under quid pro quo contributed 8.3% of the quality improvement experienced by affiliated domestic models between 2001 and 2014, relative to nonaffiliated domestic models"*

**Weitere kritische Stellen:**
- **Qualitätslücke 65% → 33%**: *"From 2001 to 2014, malfunction rates of domestically produced car models decreased by over 75%... domestic models had 65% higher malfunction rates than JV models in 2001; by 2014, this gap had reduced to 33%"* (p. 3)
- **Spillover-Koeffizient**: 0,098 SD pro 1 SD Verbesserung in JV-Qualität (p. 15, Table 3, col. 2)
- **Roewe** (p. 7): *"SAIC introduced Roewe in 2006"* — direkte Politikkonsequenz
- **SAIC-VW explizit** (p. 8): *"VW–SAIC produces Passat and Tiguan cars"*
- **Mechanismen**: Worker flows + Lieferantennetzwerke (p. 5) — NICHT Patente!

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ (Hauptargument 4.1.2):**

> The most direct causal evidence on the policy's effects on SAIC specifically is provided by Bai, Barwick, Cao and Li (2025). Exploiting within-product variation across multiple quality dimensions in the J.D. Power Initial Quality Study database covering nearly all car models produced in China between 2001 and 2014, they estimate that ownership affiliation under the quid pro quo regime contributed 8.3% of the total quality improvement experienced by affiliated domestic models, relative to non-affiliated domestic counterparts (Bai et al., 2025, p. 1). Over the same period, the quality gap between domestic and joint-venture-produced vehicles narrowed substantially: domestic models exhibited 65% higher malfunction rates than JV models in 2001, but only 33% higher by 2014 (Bai et al., 2025, p. 3). The point estimate of the spillover coefficient is 0.098 — that is, when a JV model scores one standard deviation higher on a given quality dimension, the affiliated domestic automaker's indigenous models score 0.098 standard deviations higher on the same dimension relative to models from non-affiliated domestic firms (Bai et al., 2025, p. 15, Table 3, col. 2). The transmission mechanism, the authors find, was not formal patent transfer but rather worker mobility between JVs and their affiliated domestic operations, combined with overlapping supplier networks (Bai et al., 2025, p. 5).

**ZWEITER ABSATZ — SAIC-Spezifität (sehr wichtig!):**

> Critically for the present analysis, SAIC is not a marginal case in Bai et al.'s dataset — it is a central one. SAIC Volkswagen, producing Passat and Tiguan models, and SAIC General Motors, producing Buick and Chevrolet, are both explicitly identified within the paper (Bai et al., 2025, p. 8). The authors further note that "SAIC introduced Roewe in 2006" (p. 7) — that is, the launch of SAIC's first major indigenous passenger vehicle brand is treated by the authors as a direct manifestation of the capability accumulation enabled by the JV regime. The 8.3% spillover figure can therefore be applied to SAIC with a level of confidence not available for the comparable BYD or Geely cases. For SAIC specifically, the JV mandate produced measurable, statistically identified, and causally attributable improvements in vehicle quality during the 2001–2014 window.

## 4.1.3 Howell (2018) — der Kontrapunkt (VORSICHT ABS 3!)

**Argumentkern:** Während Bai et al. positive Qualitätsspillover zeigen, dokumentiert Howell parallel den schädlichen Effekt: JV-Renten reduzierten Anreize zur autonomen Innovation.

**⚠️ WARNUNG:** Howell ist ABS 3 — KEIN A/A*-Journal. Verwende es nur in Kombination mit Bai et al., nie als alleinigen Beleg. Formulierung: "consistent with", "complements", "documents an additional dynamic" — NICHT "proves".

**Quelle:** Howell (2018), Research Policy.

**Schlüsselzahlen/Zitate:**
- Firmen mit JVs reduzierten unter 2009 Emissionsstandards: **−11% Drehmoment, −15% Preis, −65 kg Gewicht** (p. 1457, Table 2)
- *"Since 2008, private firms and local SOEs without JVs have been responsible for almost all passenger vehicle exports"* (p. 1457)
- *"The cannibalization channel appears to outweigh any benefits from knowledge spillovers in the JV"* (p. 1458)
- Wörtliches Zitat He Guangyan (ehem. Maschinenbau-Minister): *"like opium for Chinese firms"* (p. 1448)

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ (Kontrapunkt):**

> The picture, however, is not unambiguously positive. Howell (2018), exploiting China's 2009 Phase 2 fuel economy standards as a quasi-experiment, documents an effect that operates in the opposite direction. Using a difference-in-differences design comparing domestic firms with and without JV partnerships, she finds that JV-linked firms responded to the binding regulatory constraint by reducing rather than upgrading product quality: torque fell by 11%, prices by 15%, and vehicle weight by 65 kg relative to comparable firms without JVs (Howell, 2018, p. 1457). The mechanism, Howell argues, is a cannibalization dynamic: domestic firms with JV partners earn rents from the foreign brand's sales, and upgrading their own technology would compete with — and partially substitute for — that profitable JV income. The incentive to innovate is therefore structurally suppressed. As Howell observes, "the cannibalization channel appears to outweigh any benefits from knowledge spillovers in the JV" (Howell, 2018, p. 1458). The export pattern of the Chinese automotive industry supports this interpretation: since 2008, passenger vehicle exports have been almost entirely accounted for by domestic firms *without* JV partnerships (Howell, 2018, p. 1457). The cannibalization mechanism is succinctly captured by Former Minister of Machinery He Guangyan, who described JV rents as "like opium" for Chinese firms — addictive, profitable in the short term, and inimical to long-term capability development (cited in Howell, 2018, p. 1448).

**SYNTHESEABSATZ (Hier zeigst du analytische Tiefe!):**

> Taken together, the findings of Bai et al. (2025) and Howell (2018) describe a capability-building paradox rather than a contradiction. The JV model demonstrably transferred production quality — 8.3% improvement in the affiliated domestic models, narrowing the quality gap from 65% to 33% — but simultaneously suppressed the autonomous innovation effort needed to develop next-generation capabilities. SAIC's accumulated outcome reflects this duality: by 2014 the firm produced vehicles of substantially improved quality under the Roewe and MG marques, but it did not develop the proprietary battery, power electronics, or software platforms that would later define competitive advantage in the electric vehicle era. The 26,000 patents reported in SAIC's 2024 Annual Report (SAIC Motor, 2025) reflect the former achievement; the absence of a competitive proprietary EV platform comparable to BYD's Blade Battery or DM-i system reflects the latter cost.

## 4.1.4 JV-Instabilität — Inkpen & Beamish (1997)

**Argumentkern:** Das JV-Modell wird theoretisch instabil, sobald die Wissenslücke zwischen den Partnern geschlossen ist. Bai et al.'s Daten zeigen, dass dieser Moment für SAIC erreicht ist.

**Quelle:** Inkpen & Beamish (1997), AMR, ABS 4*

**Proposition 1 (p. 188):**
> *"As the foreign partner's local knowledge increases, the foreign partner's dependence on the local partner decreases, leading to a shift in bargaining power and greater likelihood of the instability of the IJV."*

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> The theoretical literature on international joint ventures predicts precisely this trajectory. Inkpen and Beamish (1997), in the standard reference on JV instability, argue that international joint ventures become unstable when one partner accumulates sufficient knowledge to render its dependence on the other partner obsolete (Inkpen & Beamish, 1997, p. 188, Proposition 1). The narrowing of the malfunction-rate gap from 65% to 33% over the 2001–2014 period documented by Bai et al. (2025) is precisely the knowledge-parity trigger this framework identifies: once Chinese domestic models approached near-parity with JV-produced models on the dimensions of established automotive quality, the original rationale for the JV — technology and capability transfer in exchange for market access — began to dissolve. The structural conditions for JV instability had emerged years before the financial crisis became visible in SAIC's 2024 results.

## 4.1.5 Aktuelle Eigenleistungen — SAIC AR 2025 (Schlussabsatz)

**Argumentkern:** SAIC hat begonnen, autonome Kapazitäten aufzubauen — aber spät und ohne Skalierungserfolg.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2025, pp. 9, 11, 16, 21.

**Datenpunkte:**
- NEV 2025: **1,643 Mio. Einheiten** (+33,14% YoY) (p. 21)
- Halbfeststoff-Batterie: **industry-first** Massenproduktion im MG4 (p. 11)
- Domestic Chip Adoption: **>30%** in SAIC-Fahrzeugen (p. 11)
- Huawei-Kooperation: **SAIC SHANGJIE H5** Markteinführung September 2025 (p. 9)
- Total R&D (inkl. JVs): RMB 33,58 Mrd. (p. 16)
- F&E-Personal: 30.329 (17,6% der Gesamtbelegschaft, p. 16)

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> SAIC's 2025 Annual Report documents the firm's accelerated response to the capability deficit. NEV sales reached 1.643 million units in 2025, an increase of 33.14% year-on-year (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 21). The firm achieved what it describes as the industry's first mass deployment of second-generation semi-solid battery technology in its MG4 model (p. 11), exceeded 30% adoption of domestically-produced semiconductors across its vehicle lineup, and launched the SAIC SHANGJIE brand in partnership with Huawei, with the first model H5 reaching market in September 2025 (p. 9). Aggregate R&D expenditure including JV operations reached RMB 33.58 billion (p. 16). These developments are substantive, but they entered commercial deployment more than a decade after BYD's equivalent investments in proprietary EV platforms began generating market position. The temporal gap between SAIC's accumulated production quality (well-documented by 2014) and its independent EV capability (emerging only in 2025) is the empirical signature of the cannibalization mechanism Howell (2018) identifies: the structural disincentive to invest in autonomous capability delayed precisely the investments SAIC now requires to compete.

# 🔮 ABSCHNITT 5.1 — LONG-TERM VIABILITY (~1 Seite)

## 5.1.1 Quantifizierter JV-Zerfall (DEIN STÄRKSTES VIABILITY-ARGUMENT)

**Argumentkern:** Die Kapazitätsauslastung der JVs zeigt strukturelle (nicht zyklische) Schwäche.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2025, pp. 19–20.

**Datenpunkte (DIREKT AUS AR 2025):**

| Werk | Designkapazität (Einheiten) | Produktion 2025 | Auslastung |
|---|---|---|---|
| SAIC Volkswagen | 1.920.000 | 1.058.000 | **55%** |
| SAIC General Motors | 1.452.000 | 534.000 | **37%** |
| SAIC Passenger Vehicle (eigen) | 1.160.000 | 885.000 | 76% |
| SAIC GM Wuling | 1.760.000 | 1.671.000 | 95% |
| **VW MEB-Elektrowerk** | **240.000** | **34.464** | **14%** |

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> The structural deterioration of SAIC's JV-based business model is quantitatively visible in capacity utilization data reported in the 2025 Annual Report. In 2025, SAIC General Motors operated at 37% of designed capacity, producing 534,000 vehicles against a designed capacity of 1.452 million (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 19). SAIC Volkswagen reached 55%, producing 1.058 million units of a designed 1.920 million. Most strikingly, Volkswagen's dedicated MEB platform plant — purpose-built for electric vehicle production with a designed capacity of 240,000 units — achieved an actual production of 34,464 vehicles in 2025, an utilization rate of 14% (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 20). Utilization figures of this magnitude do not reflect cyclical weakness or temporary supply chain disruptions; they reflect structural demand failure for the JV product mix in the Chinese market. By contrast, SAIC's domestically-operated assembly plants for own-brand vehicles operate at 76% capacity utilization (SAIC Motor Passenger Vehicle Branch) and 95% (SAIC GM Wuling, focused on low-cost EVs), indicating that the underlying production system is not impaired — it is the specific JV-foreign-brand portfolio that has lost market traction.

## 5.1.2 Marktkontext — IEA 2026

**Argumentkern:** Die Marktverschiebung ist abgeschlossen, nicht bevorstehend.

**Quelle:** IEA Global EV Outlook 2026, pp. 10, 12, 18.

**Datenpunkte:**
- China NEV-Anteil 2025: **~55%** aller Neuwagen (p. 18)
- *"Monthly electric car sales exceeded a 50% sales share in 11 out of 12 months of 2025"* (p. 18)
- Chinesische Hersteller: **60%** des globalen EV-Absatzes 2025 (p. 10)
- Prognose China: **>90%** EV-Anteil bis 2035 (p. 12)
- *"70% of battery electric cars sold in China in 2025 were already cheaper than the average conventional car"* (p. 12)

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> The market context renders the persistence of SAIC's ICE-heavy JV portfolio strategically untenable. According to the International Energy Agency, electric vehicles accounted for approximately 55% of new car sales in China during 2025, with monthly share exceeding 50% in 11 out of 12 months (IEA, 2026, p. 18). Chinese-headquartered automakers supplied 60% of global EV sales in the same year (p. 10). Of particular importance for joint-venture-dependent firms, the IEA documents that "70% of battery electric cars sold in China in 2025 were already cheaper than the average conventional car" (p. 12) — price parity has been reached, eliminating the premium-positioning rationale that supported foreign brands' market share during the ICE era. The IEA projects that EVs will exceed 90% of Chinese new car sales by 2035 (p. 12). These are not forecasts of a future transition; they are documentation of one that has substantially completed.

## 5.1.3 EU-Zoll als regulatorische Schließung

**Argumentkern:** Der wichtigste alternative Wachstumsmarkt (EU) ist mit dem höchsten Zollsatz blockiert.

**Quelle:** EU Reg. 2024/2754, Art. 1, p. 228.

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> Compounding the domestic market dislocation, SAIC's primary alternative growth channel — European exports — has been regulatorily constrained at the maximum tariff level. The European Commission's definitive countervailing duty of 35.3% applies to SAIC Motor, SAIC Volkswagen, and SAIC General Motors collectively (European Commission, 2024, Art. 1, p. 228) — equal to the rate applied to non-cooperating producers and more than double the rate imposed on BYD (17.0%). With both the domestic JV business losing share to native EV competitors and the European export channel constrained, SAIC's near-term revenue diversification options are structurally narrow.

## 5.1.4 SAIC-Antwort — Joint Venture 2.0 (Schlussabsatz)

**Argumentkern:** SAIC erkennt die Lage und reagiert mit Kapazitäts-Umwidmung — Erfolg unklar.

**Quelle:** SAIC AR 2025, p. 12.

**Faktum:** Freigesetzte JV-Kapazität wird für Eigenmarken (SAIC SHANGJIE) genutzt. SAIC-VW JV-Verlängerung bis 2040 + 18 neue Modelle bis 2030.

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> SAIC's strategic response, articulated in its 2025 Annual Report as the "Joint Venture 2.0" model, involves reallocating freed-up JV manufacturing capacity to own-brand electric vehicle production, with the SAIC SHANGJIE brand developed jointly with Huawei serving as the flagship of this transition (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 12). Simultaneously, the SAIC-VW joint venture was extended in 2024 to 2040, with VW committing to 18 new models by 2030 (SAIC Motor, 2025). Whether this dual-track strategy — JV transformation alongside accelerated own-brand development — can compensate for the structural decline in JV profitability remains the central open question for SAIC's long-term viability. The framework provided by Inkpen and Beamish (1997) suggests that the deeper challenge is not strategic but structural: once knowledge parity between JV partners has been reached, the original rationale for the JV no longer holds, and the partnership becomes contractually preserved rather than economically generative. SAIC's resilience in the EV-transition era will therefore depend less on the success of any particular product launch and more on whether the firm can complete the transition from a JV-rent-dependent business model to an autonomous-capability business model before the existing JV income base further erodes.

# 🎓 ABSCHNITT 6 — LESSONS (SAIC-Beiträge zu den Generalizable Lessons)

Du musst hier nur SAIC-spezifische Lektionen liefern, das Synthese-Schreiben mit Batu/Geely-Kollege macht ihr gemeinsam.

## Lesson 1 — JV-Renten zerstören Innovationsanreize

**Konstruktion:** Du verbindest die Bai-Howell-Spannung mit Inkpen-Beamish.

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> The SAIC case illustrates a capability-building paradox inherent in mandatory technology transfer regimes. Joint venture affiliation generated measurable improvements in production quality — an 8.3% spillover documented causally by Bai et al. (2025) — but simultaneously suppressed the incentive for autonomous innovation through the rent cannibalization mechanism identified by Howell (2018). Once the knowledge gap between foreign and domestic partners narrowed (from a 65% malfunction-rate differential in 2001 to 33% in 2014, per Bai et al.), the structural rationale for the JV partnership eroded — exactly the dynamic predicted by Inkpen and Beamish (1997, Proposition 1). The lesson generalizes beyond the Chinese context: forced technology transfer arrangements should be evaluated not only by what they transfer but by what indigenous innovation they displace, and by the timeline over which the transferred capability becomes obsolete relative to the next technological frontier.

---

## Lesson 2 — Governance-Struktur determiniert Anpassungsgeschwindigkeit

**ENGLISCHER MUSTERABSATZ:**

> Governance structure is not a peripheral concern but a first-order determinant of strategic adaptability in fast-moving markets. SAIC's formally codified Party leadership mandate (SAIC Motor, 2026, p. 35), combined with the empirical evidence that politically connected firms in China systematically underperform on three-year post-IPO returns by approximately 18% (Fan et al., 2007, p. 340), suggests that the pace of SAIC's EV transition has been constrained not by absence of resources but by competing political objectives — employment preservation, JV-partner relationship continuity, and intra-party political signalling — that operate above commercial logic in the firm's decision architecture (Shleifer & Vishny, 1994, Proposition 13, p. 1020). The generalizable lesson is that in industries undergoing rapid technological transition, governance structures that include strong political principals tend to optimize for stability rather than disruption, even when disruption is the strategically dominant option.
